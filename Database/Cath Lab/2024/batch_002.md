## Q51
Question: All of the following are techniques for determination of cardiac output EXCEPT:
Option A: Thermodilution method
Option B: Manometric method
Option C: Indicator dilution method
Option D: Fick oxygen method
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The manometric method is used to measure fluid pressures (like invasive blood pressure), not cardiac output. Thermodilution, Fick's method, and indicator dilution are standard methods for quantifying cardiac output.
## Q52
Question: Mixed venous oxygen content is derived using the following:
Option A: Dexter's criteria
Option B: Hung's formula
Option C: Fick's equation
Option D: Flamm's formula
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Flamm's equation is used to calculate true mixed venous oxygen saturation from samples taken in the vena cavae, specifically formula: (3/4 \times \text{SVC}) + (1/4 \times \text{IVC}).
## Q53
Question: In oximetry run all of the following are causes of significant step up at atrial level EXCEPT:
Option A: Coronary fistula to RV
Option B: RSOV to RA
Option C: PAPVC
Option D: VSD with TR
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: A coronary fistula draining into the Right Ventricle (RV) will cause a step-up in oxygen saturation at the ventricular level, not the atrial level.
## Q54
Question: The Gorlin formula for valve area estimation is based on which hemodynamic principle:
Option A: Poiseuille's law
Option B: Wood's principle
Option C: Torricelli's law
Option D: Laplace's law
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: The Gorlin formula uses Torricelli’s law of orifice flow, which relates the velocity of fluid flowing out of an orifice to the pressure gradient across it.
## Q55
Question: Which of the following is an angiographic measure of myocardial perfusion at capillary level:
Option A: TIMI flow grade
Option B: TIMI frame count
Option C: Coronary venous efflux
Option D: Myocardial blush score
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: While TIMI flow grades the epicardial coronary blood flow, the Myocardial Blush Grade (MBG) or score specifically assesses the microvascular/capillary level perfusion of the myocardial tissue.
## Q56
Question: All are methods of invasive intracoronary physiological assessment EXCEPT:
Option A: QFR
Option B: iFR
Option C: CFR
Option D: FFR
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Quantitative Flow Ratio (QFR) is a novel method for estimating FFR based on 3D quantitative coronary angiography and fluid dynamics. It is considered "non-invasive" (or wire-free) compared to iFR, CFR, and FFR, which require introducing a pressure wire into the coronary artery.
## Q57
Question: All are intravascular imaging modalities EXCEPT:
Option A: IVUS
Option B: IVL
Option C: OCT
Option D: NIRS
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: IVL stands for Intravascular Lithotripsy, which is a therapeutic technique using sonic waves to crack intimal/medial calcium. IVUS, OCT, and NIRS (Near-Infrared Spectroscopy) are all diagnostic imaging modalities.
## Q58
Question: Following are the current indications of Endomyocardial biopsy except:
Option A: Cardiac allograft rejection
Option B: Restrictive heart disease
Option C: Takotsubo cardiomyopathy
Option D: Anthracyclin cardiotoxicity
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Takotsubo (stress-induced) cardiomyopathy is a transient condition typically diagnosed via echocardiography and coronary angiography (to rule out ACS). An invasive endomyocardial biopsy is generally not indicated or required for its diagnosis.
## Q59
Question: All are contraindications of IABP EXCEPT:
Option A: Aortic dissection
Option B: Severe AR
Option C: Previous Ischemic CVA
Option D: Abdominal aortic aneurysm
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Severe aortic regurgitation (AR), aortic dissection, and large abdominal aortic aneurysms are absolute contraindications for Intra-Aortic Balloon Pump (IABP) placement. A previous ischemic stroke (CVA) is not an absolute contraindication.
## Q60
Question: All are novel techniques of coronary calcium modification in PCI EXCEPT:
Option A: Rotablation
Option B: Intravascular lithotripsy
Option C: Orbital atherectomy
Option D: Penumbra system
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The Penumbra system is a mechanical aspiration thrombectomy device used to remove blood clots, whereas Rotablation, IVL, and Orbital atherectomy are specifically designed to modify heavy coronary calcifications.
## Q61
Question: PCWP tracing differs from direct LA pressure tracing by all of the following EXCEPT:
Option A: Waves occur earlier in time than LA waveforms
Option B: 'c' waves maybe absent
Option C: Damped nature than LA trace
Option D: Slightly lower pressures than LA pressure
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Pulmonary Capillary Wedge Pressure (PCWP) waveforms are actually *delayed* in time compared to direct Left Atrial (LA) waveforms due to the time it takes the pressure wave to travel backward through the pulmonary capillary bed.
## Q62
Question: Following are hemodynamic hallmarks of Constrictive pericarditis EXCEPT:
Option A: Square root sign
Option B: Reduced ventricular interdependence
Option C: Equalisation of diastolic pressures
Option D: Friedrich's sign
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Constrictive pericarditis is classically characterized by *enhanced* ventricular interdependence (e.g., discordant pressure changes in the LV and RV during respiration), not reduced.
## Q63
Question: All are methods to reduce radiation exposure to operator in cathlab EXCEPT:
Option A: Using more steeper cranial or caudal imaging views
Option B: Increasing distance from radiation source
Option C: Collimation
Option D: Minimising cine frame rates
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Steeper angulations (extreme cranial or caudal views) require higher radiation doses to penetrate more tissue and they generate significantly more scatter radiation, increasing operator exposure.
## Q64
Question: Autoclaving and microwaving are done for which of the following types of medical wastes?
Option A: Microbiological waste

Option D: Human anatomical waste
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Recyclable contaminated plastics (Red bag waste) like catheters and IV tubes are typically treated by autoclaving or microwaving to sterilize them before sending them for shredding and recycling.
## Q65

Option A: Made of non-chlorinated plastic material

## Q66
Question: What is the percentage of shepherd crook RCA?
Option A: 5%
Option B: 10%
Option C: 15%
Option D: 20%
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: The "Shepherd's crook" is an anatomical variant of the right coronary artery featuring an extreme upward course followed by a sharp hairpin turn. It is observed in approximately 5% of patients.
## Q67

Explanation: The anterolateral papillary muscle generally has a dual blood supply from both the diagonal branches of the Left Anterior Descending (LAD) artery and the obtuse marginal branches of the Left Circumflex (LCx). The official key selects Option D.
## Q68
Question: Anterior muscular defects in ventricular septum are best demonstrated by:

Explanation: During a left ventriculogram, the Right Anterior Oblique (RAO) view is best for visualizing the anterior and apical muscular portions of the ventricular septum.

## Q70

## Q71
Question: Inverse square law states that intensity of radiation is:
Option A: Directly proportional to the distance
Option B: Directly proportional to the square of the distance
Option C: Inversely proportional to the distance
Option D: Inversely proportional to the square of the distance
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: A core principle of radiation safety, the inverse square law states that the intensity of the x-ray beam is inversely proportional to the square of the distance from the source. Doubling your distance reduces exposure to one-fourth.

## Q73
Question: During IABP balloon deflation should occur at:
Option A: Mid of systole
Option B: End of systole
Option C: Before the onset of systole
Option D: Balloon should not be deflated
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The Intra-Aortic Balloon Pump (IABP) must deflate precisely *before* or right at the onset of ventricular systole (timed with the R wave on ECG) to reduce afterload and allow the left ventricle to eject blood easily.
## Q74
Question: What is the sensitivity of Allen's test?
Option A: 25%
Option B: 55%
Option C: 75%
Option D: 90%
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: The modified Allen's test is used to assess collateral circulation before radial artery puncture. Clinical studies have shown it has relatively low sensitivity (approx. 54-55%) for detecting abnormal ulnar collateral flow.
## Q75
Question: How many end holes does an NIH catheter have?
Option A: 1
Option B: 2
Option C: 3
Option D: 0
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The National Institutes of Health (NIH) catheter is a closed-end catheter with multiple side holes (usually 4 to 6). It is designed to deliver high-flow contrast without catheter recoil or myocardial staining.
## Q76
Question: Intravascular lithotripsy uses:
Option A: Ultrasound
Option B: Sonic waves
Option C: X ray
Option D: Gamma radiation
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Intravascular Lithotripsy (IVL) relies on acoustic shockwaves (sonic pressure waves) delivered via a specialized balloon to fracture hard calcium inside the coronary artery walls.
## Q77
Question: Which among the following is a low osmolar contrast media?
Option A: Iothalamate

## Q78
Question: Distal landmark used for TIMI frame count in Right coronary artery:

Explanation: The corrected TIMI frame count is a standardized method for assessing coronary flow. For the RCA, the designated distal landmark is the first branch of the posterolateral artery.
## Q79
Question: In sarcoidosis diagnostic yield of endomyocardial biopsy is:
Option A: 10-20%
Option B: 20-30%
Option C: 30-40%
Option D: 40-50%
Correct Answer: Option B

Explanation: Due to the patchy and focal nature of granuloma distribution in the myocardium, the diagnostic yield of a blind endomyocardial biopsy for cardiac sarcoidosis is notably low, typically around 20-30%.
## Q80
Question: An a wave equal to V wave in left atrial pressure waveform may be due to:
Option A: Cardiac tamponade
Option B: Constrictive pericarditis
Option C: Both (1) and (2)
Option D: None of the above
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: In both cardiac tamponade and constrictive pericarditis, intracardiac pressures equalize across all chambers during diastole, resulting in prominent and equalized 'a' and 'v' waves in atrial tracings.
## Q81
Question: Saw-toothed atrial activity in the electrocardiogram (ECG), is seen in:
Option A: Paroxysmal atrial tachycardia
Option B: Atrial fibrillation
Option C: AV Nodal re-entrant tachycardia
Option D: Atrial flutter
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Atrial flutter is a macro-reentrant arrhythmia characterized by a distinct "saw-tooth" pattern of flutter waves (F waves), typically at a rate of 250-350 bpm.
## Q82
Question: In AV nodal re-entrant tachycardia, electrophysiological ablation is done in the area of:
Option A: Slow pathway
Option B: Cavo-tricuspid isthmus
Option C: Crista terminalis
Option D: Fast pathway
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: AVNRT is caused by a dual pathway physiology in the AV node. The highly successful and safe therapeutic target for catheter ablation is the slow pathway.
## Q83
Question: Implantable cardioverter defibrillators distinguish between ventricular tachycardia and ventricular fibrillation by:
Option A: AV dissociation
Option B: QRS rate
Option C: QRS amplitude
Option D: P amplitude
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: ICDs primarily use programmable rate zones to differentiate arrhythmias. Tachycardias falling in the extremely fast rate zone (e.g., >200-250 bpm) are classified and treated as Ventricular Fibrillation (VF).
## Q84

## Q85
Question: Delta waves in the electrocardiogram is indicative of:
Option A: Hypokalemia
Option B: Dual AV node
Option C: Accessory AV pathway
Option D: Rheumatic fever
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: A delta wave (slurred upstroke of the QRS) indicates that ventricular activation is occurring prematurely through an abnormal accessory pathway (bundle of Kent), as seen in WPW syndrome. *(Note: Corrected spelling from "Deta waves").*
## Q86
Question: During electrophysiological study, during atrial extra stimulus, an AH jump indicates:
Option A: Accessory AV pathway
Option B: AV nodal dual pathway
Option C: Absent AV nodal conduction
Option D: Clockwise atrial flutter
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: An AH jump (a sudden increase in the AH interval by ≥ 50 ms for a 10 ms decrement in coupling interval) confirms dual AV node physiology, shifting conduction from the fast pathway to the slow pathway.
## Q87
Question: Capture beats are characteristic of:
Option A: AV nodal re-entrant tachycardia
Option B: Ventricular fibrillation
Option C: Atrial fibrillation
Option D: Ventricular tachycardia
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: In Ventricular Tachycardia (VT), the presence of capture beats (and fusion beats) acts as strong evidence of AV dissociation, helping to differentiate VT from SVT with aberrancy.
## Q88
Question: Which is true about complete heart block?
Option A: There is a constant relation between QRS and P waves
Option B: Drugs like beta blockers are useful
Option C: There is AV dissociation
Option D: QRS rate is faster than p rate
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: In third-degree (complete) heart block, no atrial impulses reach the ventricles, resulting in complete Atrioventricular (AV) dissociation where the atria and ventricles beat independently.
## Q89
Question: AAI permanent pacemaker was used for:
Option A: Atrial fibrillation
Option B: Sick sinus syndrome
Option C: Complete heart block
Option D: Implantable cardioverter defibrillator
Correct Answer: Option B

## Q90
Question: During ventricular pacing in electrophysiological study, an eccentric retrograde activation noted in the coronary sinus catheter indicates:
Option A: Left lateral AV pathway
Option B: Septal pathway
Option C: Right lateral AV pathway
Option D: Normal retrograde activation pattern
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Normal retrograde activation (via the AV node) is concentric (earliest at the His). Eccentric activation (earliest signal out in the distal coronary sinus) indicates conduction up a left lateral accessory pathway.
## Q91
Question: Synchronized DC shock should not be given in:
Option A: Ventricular tachycardia
Option B: Ventricular fibrillation
Option C: Atrial flutter
Option D: Atrial fibrillation
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Ventricular fibrillation generates chaotic electrical activity with no distinct R wave. Attempting a synchronized shock will cause the machine to indefinitely delay therapy. VF requires an immediate *unsynchronized* shock (defibrillation).
## Q92
Question: Drug of choice in severe anaphylaxis:
Option A: Adrenalin
Option B: Hydrocortisone
Option C: Noradrenalin
Option D: Isoprenalin

Explanation: Adrenaline (Epinephrine) administered intramuscularly is the primary, life-saving drug of choice to reverse the severe vasodilation and bronchoconstriction of anaphylaxis.
## Q93
Question: Cardiac tamponade is relieved by:
Option A: Diuretics
Option B: DC shock
Option C: Pressure controlled ventilation
Option D: Aspiration of pericardial fluid
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Cardiac tamponade is a mechanical compression of the heart by fluid in the pericardial space. It is treated by immediate needle aspiration (pericardiocentesis) or surgical drainage. Diuretics are contraindicated.

## Q95
Question: Which is a contraindication for intra-aortic balloon pump?
Option A: Multivessel percutaneous coronary intervention
Option B: Left main stem coronary lesion
Option C: Severe aortic regurgitation
Option D: Low cardiac output
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Severe aortic regurgitation is an absolute contraindication for an IABP because balloon inflation in diastole will massively increase regurgitant flow back into the left ventricle, causing rapid failure.
## Q96

## Q97
Question: The balloon of the Intra aortic balloon pump is inflated using:
Option A: Carbon dioxide
Option B: Oxygen
Option C: Helium
Option D: Air
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Helium gas is used because it has a very low density, allowing it to rapidly shuttle in and out of the balloon to match the patient's heart rate. It is also easily absorbed in the blood if a small leak occurs.

## Q99
Question: Dreaded complication of anaphylaxis is:
Option A: Seizure
Option B: Renal failure

## Q100
Question: Basic life support includes:
Option A: Intubation and ventilation
Option B: Chest compressions
Option C: Intravenous adrenalin
Option D: Defibrillation
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Basic Life Support (BLS) focuses on immediate recognition of arrest, early CPR (chest compressions), and use of an AED. Intubation, IV drugs, and manual defibrillation fall under Advanced Cardiovascular Life Support (ACLS).
