## Q51
Question: What change in hemodynamic tracing is produced if there is an air bubble in LV pressure line?
Option A: Exaggerated systolic and diastolic over shoot
Option B: Overdamping of tracing
Option C: Systolic overshoot only, diastole unaffected
Option D: No change
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Small air bubbles in the fluid-filled tubing system increase the compliance of the system, lowering its natural resonant frequency. If this resonant frequency approaches the heart rate harmonics, it causes underdamping (ringing), leading to an exaggerated systolic and diastolic overshoot. (Note: Very large air bubbles can cause overdamping, but the official key selects Option A).
## Q52
Question: In which condition you see LV pressure lower than aortic pressure?
Option A: Loose tubing connection
Option B: Heterotopic transplant
Option C: Extra cardiac hemodynamic support
Option D: All the above
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Left Ventricular (LV) pressure can appear lower than aortic pressure in situations where the LV is unloaded artificially (like with ECMO or Impella support), when a heterotopic heart transplant is augmenting aortic pressure, or artifactually due to a loose tubing connection damping the LV trace.
## Q53
Question: For every waveform, the LA pressure rise precedes that of PCWP by approximately:
Option A: 75 to 100 msec
Option B: 100 to 150 msec
Option C: 30 to 75 msec
Option D: 150 to 170 msec
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The Pulmonary Capillary Wedge Pressure (PCWP) is a delayed reflection of Left Atrial (LA) pressure. Because the pressure wave must travel backward through the pulmonary capillary bed, the PCWP tracing is delayed by approximately 100 to 150 milliseconds compared to the direct LA pressure tracing.
## Q54
Question: The W or M pattern in RA pressure tracing is seen in:
Option A: Tricuspid stenosis
Option B: VSD with severe TR
Option C: Constrictive pericarditis
Option D: Restrictive cardiomyopathy
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: In constrictive pericarditis, the Right Atrial (RA) pressure tracing characteristically shows prominent 'a' and 'v' waves with rapid, deep 'x' and 'y' descents, creating a classic "W" or "M" pattern.
## Q55
Question: Brockenbrough-Braunwald-Morrow phenomenon is seen during catheterisation of a case of:
Option A: Constrictive pericarditis
Option B: Restrictive cardiomyopathy
Option C: HOCM
Option D: Both B) and C)
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The Brockenbrough-Braunwald-Morrow sign is a paradoxical decrease in aortic pulse pressure following a premature ventricular contraction (PVC). It is a classic hemodynamic hallmark of Hypertrophic Obstructive Cardiomyopathy (HOCM).
## Q56
Question: Which of the following right heart catheterisation data is consistent with diagnosis of Primary Pulmonary Hypertension?
Option A: mPAP > 25 mm Hg, PCWP > 15 mm Hg, PVR > 3 Wood units
Option B: mPAP > 25 mm Hg, PCWP < 15 mm Hg, PVR > 3 Wood units
Option C: mPAP > 25 mm Hg, PCWP > 15 mm Hg, PVR < 3 Wood units
Option D: mPAP > 25 mm Hg, PCWP < 25 mm Hg, PVR < 3 Wood units
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Primary Pulmonary Hypertension (Group 1 PAH) is a pre-capillary condition defined by a mean Pulmonary Artery Pressure (mPAP) > 25 mmHg, a normal Pulmonary Capillary Wedge Pressure (PCWP) < 15 mmHg, and a Pulmonary Vascular Resistance (PVR) > 3 Wood units.

## Q58
Question: Pacemaker Syndrome occur in patients with:
Option A: VVI pacemaker only
Option B: DDD pacemaker only
Option C: Both VVI and DDD pacemakers
Option D: VVI pacemaker with atrial fibrillation
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Pacemaker syndrome is the clinical consequence of AV dyssynchrony. While it most classically occurs in patients with a VVI pacemaker and intact retrograde ventriculoatrial conduction, it can also occur in patients with a DDD pacemaker if it is suboptimally programmed (e.g., excessively long AV delays). *(Note: The official provisional key marked Option B, but Option C is the most clinically accurate answer).*
## Q59
Question: Pacemaker Mediated Tachycardia (PMT) can be terminated with:
Option A: Application of a magnet over PG
Option B: Retrograde V-A conduction block
Option C: Programming longer PVARP
Option D: All the above
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: PMT is a re-entrant tachycardia driven by a dual-chamber pacemaker. It can be acutely terminated by applying a magnet (which asynchronousizes the pacemaker), or prevented by programming a longer Post-Ventricular Atrial Refractory Period (PVARP) or establishing retrograde V-A block.
## Q60
Question: Normal H-V interval is:
Option A: 30 – 55 msec
Option B: 50 – 150 msec
Option C: 10 – 25 msec
Option D: 170 – 290 msec
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: In an electrophysiological study, the H-V interval measures conduction time from the His bundle to the ventricular myocardium. The normal range is 35 to 55 milliseconds (Option A is the closest fit).
## Q61
Question: A regular narrow QRS tachycardia with a rate of 150 in a patient with rheumatic heart disease is most likely:
Option A: Atrial fibrillation
Option B: Atrial flutter
Option C: Antidromic AVRT
Option D: Fascicular VT
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: A regular narrow complex tachycardia at exactly 150 bpm is the classic presentation of Atrial Flutter with 2:1 AV block (atrial rate 300 bpm, ventricular rate 150 bpm). Enlarged atria from rheumatic heart disease strongly predispose patients to atrial flutter.

## Q63
Question: During IABP, balloon inflation should occur at:
Option A: R wave of ECG
Option B: Tidal wave of aortic tracing
Option C: ST segment of ECG
Option D: Dicrotic notch of aortic tracing
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: To maximize diastolic augmentation and coronary perfusion, the Intra-Aortic Balloon Pump (IABP) must inflate precisely at the onset of diastole, which corresponds hemodynamically to the dicrotic notch (closure of the aortic valve) on the arterial pressure tracing.
## Q64

Option A: IABP
Option B: ECMO

Explanation: Extracorporeal Membrane Oxygenation (ECMO) provides complete biventricular and respiratory (cardiopulmonary) support, taking over the full function of the heart and lungs, unlike the IABP or Impella which only offer partial left ventricular support.

## Q69
Question: Which is the correct statement regarding Left Main intervention?
Option A: Provisional stenting is adequate in most cases rather than routine 2 stent strategy
Option B: Culotte Technique is preferred over provisional stenting
Option C: T and small Protrusion is preferred over provisional stenting

## Q70

## Q71
Question: Chronic Total Occlusion (CTO) is defined as known duration of coronary occlusion more than:
Option A: One month
Option B: Three months

## Q72
Question: An operator is doing LAD-CTO angioplasty with an Amplatz guide in power position. Patient suddenly become restless with tachycardia, severe hypotension and tachypnoea but no chest pain. ECG monitor doesn’t show much ischemic changes. Echo doesn’t show pericardial effusion. The anxious operator after taking a check angio find it difficult to arrive at a conclusion. You have to warn the operator for possible:
Option A: Acute Mitral regurgitation
Option B: Acute Aortic dissection
Option C: Acute Aortic regurgitation
Option D: Left main dissection
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Amplatz guiding catheters deeply engage the coronary ostium and sit heavily on the aortic valve cusps. Aggressive manipulation can inadvertently damage or tear the aortic valve leaflet, causing acute severe aortic regurgitation. This presents with sudden severe hypotension, tachycardia, and pulmonary edema (tachypnea) without pericardial effusion or immediate massive ECG ischemia.

## Q79
Question: While doing a bifurcation angioplasty, the size of main vessel stent must be preferably the size of:

Option B: Diameter of main vessel distal to the bifurcation
Option C: (A + B)/2

Correct Answer: Option B

## Q80
Question: For a Medina 1.1.1 bifurcation lesion, if the size of distal branches are 3 mm each, the minimum size of the proximal vessel needed for doing Simultaneous Kissing Stenting (SKS) is:
Option A: 3 mm
Option B: 3.6 mm
Option C: 4.2 mm
Option D: 4.8 mm
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Based on Murray's Law and Finet's formula for coronary bifurcations (D_{proximal} = 0.678 \times (D_{distal1} + D_{distal2})), if both distal branches are 3.0 mm, the minimum proximal vessel diameter accommodating both is roughly 0.7 \times (3 + 3) = 4.2 \text{ mm}.
## Q81

## Q91
Question: During RF ablation of AVNRT, dual AV nodal physiology is suggested by:
Option A: AH jump more than 50 msec for a decrease of 10 msec S1-S2 interval

## Q100
Question: Resting coronary flow diminishes when the stenosis severity is:
Option A: ≥ 50%
Option B: ≥ 60%
Option C: ≥ 70%
Option D: ≥ 90%
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Coronary Flow Reserve (CFR) begins to drop when an epicardial stenosis reaches 50-70%. However, because of compensatory microvascular vasodilation, actual *resting* coronary blood flow does not significantly diminish until the stenosis reaches critical severity, typically ≥ 85-90%.
