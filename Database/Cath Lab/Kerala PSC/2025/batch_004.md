Question:
Which factor is most critical in maintaining perfusion pressure during Cardio Pulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) for cardiac arrest?
Option A:
Diastolic aortic pressure
Option B:
Systolic aortic pressure
Option C:
Left ventricular end systolic volume
Option D:
Right atrial pressure
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Coronary perfusion pressure is the difference between diastolic aortic pressure and right atrial pressure. Since coronary flow occurs primarily during diastole, maintaining an adequate diastolic aortic pressure is the most critical factor for ensuring coronary artery perfusion during CPR.
