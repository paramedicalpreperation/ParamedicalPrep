## Q1
**Question:** What is the phenomenon of dissipation of energy oscillation of a pressure measurement system due to friction called?
 * **Option A:** Damping
 * **Option B:** Resonance
 * **Option C:** Aliasing
 * **Option D:** Amplification
   **Correct Answer:** Option A
   **Difficulty:** Medium
   **Explanation:** Damping refers to the loss of energy in an oscillating system due to frictional forces (like fluid friction in a catheter). Proper damping is essential in invasive pressure monitoring to prevent artificial systolic overshoot.
## Q2
**Question:** In an ECG, the Wilson Central Terminal (WCT) is used as a reference point for:
 * **Option A:** Bipolar limb leads
 * **Option B:** Augmented limb leads
 * **Option C:** Unipolar precordial leads
 * **Option D:** All of the above
   **Correct Answer:** Option C
   **Difficulty:** Medium
   **Explanation:** The Wilson Central Terminal is an electrical reference point (zero potential) created by averaging the voltages of the three limb electrodes, which serves as the negative input for all six unipolar precordial (V) leads.
## Q3
**Question:** The rate pressure product (RPP) is calculated as:
 * **Option A:** Heart Rate × Systolic Blood Pressure
 * **Option B:** Cardiac Output × Heart Rate
 * **Option C:** Systolic Blood Pressure / Heart Rate
 * **Option D:** Heart Rate × Diastolic Blood Pressure
   **Correct Answer:** Option A
   **Difficulty:** Easy
   **Explanation:** The Rate Pressure Product (RPP) is a non-invasive index of myocardial oxygen demand, calculated by multiplying the heart rate by the systolic blood pressure.
## Q4
**Question:** Which of the following is an absolute contraindication for exercise stress testing?
 * **Option A:** Hypertension
 * **Option B:** Acute Myocardial Infarction within 2-3 days
 * **Option C:** Stable angina
 * **Option D:** History of TIA
   **Correct Answer:** Option B
   **Difficulty:** Easy
   **Explanation:** An acute myocardial infarction is an absolute contraindication for exercise stress testing because the heart muscle is in a vulnerable state and physical exertion can lead to further damage, rupture, or fatal arrhythmias.
## Q5
**Question:** In the Doppler equation, the Doppler shift is:
 * **Option A:** Inversely proportional to the cosine of the angle of incidence
 * **Option B:** Directly proportional to the frequency of the transducer
 * **Option C:** Directly proportional to the velocity of sound in tissue
 * **Option D:** Inversely proportional to the velocity of blood flow
   **Correct Answer:** Option B
   **Difficulty:** Hard
   **Explanation:** The Doppler shift (\Delta f = 2 \cdot f_0 \cdot v \cdot \cos\theta / c) is directly proportional to the transmitted transducer frequency (f_0) and the velocity of blood (v).
## Q6
**Question:** What is the primary purpose of using a heparinized saline flush in a catheter line?
 * **Option A:** To reduce radiation scatter
 * **Option B:** To prevent clot formation (thrombosis) within the catheter
 * **Option C:** To calibrate the pressure transducer
 * **Option D:** To increase the viscosity of contrast media
   **Correct Answer:** Option B
   **Difficulty:** Easy
   **Explanation:** Heparinized saline is used to provide continuous, slow irrigation of the catheter lumen, preventing blood from entering the catheter, clotting, and potentially causing an embolism.
## Q7
**Question:** A "step-up" in oxygen saturation during an oximetry run in the Right Ventricle indicates:
 * **Option A:** Atrial Septal Defect (ASD)
 * **Option B:** Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD)
 * **Option C:** Patent Ductus Arteriosus (PDA)
 * **Option D:** Pulmonary Stenosis
   **Correct Answer:** Option B
   **Difficulty:** Medium
   **Explanation:** A "step-up" refers to a significant increase in oxygen saturation compared to the previous chamber. A significant rise in saturation when moving from the right atrium to the right ventricle indicates a left-to-right shunt at the ventricular level (VSD).
## Q8
**Question:** The "Square Root Sign" (dip and plateau) in a ventricular pressure tracing is characteristic of:
 * **Option A:** Dilated Cardiomyopathy
 * **Option B:** Constrictive Pericarditis
 * **Option C:** Aortic Stenosis
 * **Option D:** Mitral Regurgitation
   **Correct Answer:** Option B
   **Difficulty:** Hard
   **Explanation:** The dip-and-plateau pattern (square root sign) occurs because the stiff, constricted pericardium limits ventricular filling early in diastole, causing a rapid rise in pressure that hits a ceiling.
## Q9
**Question:** Which of the following contrast media is iso-osmolar?
 * **Option A:** Iothalamate
 * **Option B:** Iohexol
 * **Option C:** Iodixanol
 * **Option D:** Iopamidol
   **Correct Answer:** Option C
   **Difficulty:** Medium
   **Explanation:** Iodixanol is a non-ionic dimeric contrast agent that is iso-osmolar to human blood, which helps reduce the risk of contrast-induced nephropathy and injection-related side effects.
## Q10
**Question:** During cardiac resuscitation, the recommended chest compression depth for adults is:
 * **Option A:** 2-3 cm
 * **Option B:** 5-6 cm
 * **Option C:** 8-9 cm
 * **Option D:** 1-2 cm
   **Correct Answer:** Option B
   **Difficulty:** Easy
   **Explanation:** High-quality CPR guidelines recommend compressing the adult chest to a depth of at least 5 cm but no more than 6 cm to ensure effective cardiac output without causing excessive injury.
## Q11
**Question:** What does a "c" wave in a central venous pressure (CVP) tracing represent?
 * **Option A:** Atrial contraction
 * **Option B:** Ventricular contraction pushing the tricuspid valve into the atrium
 * **Option C:** Passive atrial filling
 * **Option D:** Rapid ventricular filling
   **Correct Answer:** Option B
   **Difficulty:** Hard
   **Explanation:** The 'c' wave is caused by the closure and bulging of the tricuspid valve back into the right atrium during the onset of ventricular contraction (isovolumetric contraction).
## Q12
**Question:** Which modality uses NIR (Near-Infrared) light to image coronary arteries?
 * **Option A:** IVUS
 * **Option B:** OCT
 * **Option C:** Coronary Angiography
 * **Option D:** FFR
   **Correct Answer:** Option B
   **Difficulty:** Easy
   **Explanation:** Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) uses light waves (NIR) to generate high-resolution cross-sectional images of the coronary artery wall, offering much higher resolution than IVUS (which uses sound).
## Q13
**Question:** The most common location for a coronary artery aneurysm is:
 * **Option A:** Left Main
 * **Option B:** Right Coronary Artery
 * **Option C:** Left Anterior Descending
 * **Option D:** Circumflex
   **Correct Answer:** Option B
   **Difficulty:** Medium
   **Explanation:** While aneurysms can occur in any coronary artery, they are most frequently identified in the Right Coronary Artery (RCA), often as a sequela of Kawasaki disease or atherosclerosis.

 * **Option A:** Operating the X-ray console
 * **Option B:** Maintaining the sterile field and assisting with catheter/wire handling

## Q15
**Question:** The standard view to visualize the "Spider" view in coronary angiography is:
 * **Option A:** RAO Cranial
 * **Option B:** LAO Caudal
 * **Option C:** AP Caudal
 * **Option D:** RAO Caudal
   **Correct Answer:** Option B
   **Difficulty:** Medium
   **Explanation:** The LAO Caudal projection (often termed the "spider view") is specifically utilized to profile the Left Main Coronary Artery bifurcation, separating the LAD and LCx origins.
## Q16
**Question:** A sudden drop in systolic blood pressure during balloon inflation in an angioplasty usually indicates:
 * **Option A:** Successful reperfusion
 * **Option B:** Ischemia due to vessel occlusion by the balloon
 * **Option C:** A faulty pressure injector
 * **Option D:** Normal patient response
   **Correct Answer:** Option B
   **Difficulty:** Easy
   **Explanation:** When a balloon occludes the coronary artery, it cuts off distal blood flow. This causes immediate myocardial ischemia, which is often reflected by a drop in systemic blood pressure and the onset of ischemic pain on the ECG.
## Q17
**Question:** Which of the following wires is commonly used for "cross-over" techniques in bifurcation stenting?
 * **Option A:** Hydrophilic wire
 * **Option B:** Workhorse wire
 * **Option C:** CTO wire
 * **Option D:** Pressure wire
   **Correct Answer:** Option B
   **Difficulty:** Hard
   **Explanation:** In bifurcation stenting, a "workhorse" wire is typically used to wire the side branch and main branch before stenting to maintain access to both throughout the procedure.
## Q18
**Question:** The "J-curve" on a guidewire tip is designed to:
 * **Option A:** Pierce through plaques
 * **Option B:** Prevent vessel perforation and trauma during navigation
 * **Option C:** Measure pressure gradients
 * **Option D:** Deliver electrical shocks
   **Correct Answer:** Option B
   **Difficulty:** Medium
   **Explanation:** The curved or "J-tip" allows the wire to slide along the vessel lumen without the sharp tip gouging into the intima or causing dissections, making it safer for navigation through tortuous arteries.

 * **Option A:** Lead-lined aprons

## Q20
**Question:** Which device is used for LAA (Left Atrial Appendage) closure?
 * **Option A:** Stent
 * **Option B:** WATCHMAN
 * **Option C:** Amplatzer duct occluder
 * **Option D:** Coil

## Q21
**Question:** What does the 'S' wave represent in Tissue Doppler Imaging (TDI)?
 * **Option A:** Early diastole
 * **Option B:** Atrial kick (late diastole)
 * **Option C:** Systolic contraction of the myocardium
 * **Option D:** Isovolumic relaxation
   **Correct Answer:** Option C
   **Difficulty:** Medium
   **Explanation:** In TDI of the mitral annulus, the 'S' wave represents the velocity of the annulus during myocardial contraction (systole), while 'e' and 'a' waves represent diastolic motion.
## Q22
**Question:** A hemodynamically significant stenosis typically results in a FFR (Fractional Flow Reserve) value of:
 * **Option A:** > 0.90
 * **Option B:** < 0.80
 * **Option C:** 1.0
 * **Option D:** 0.85
   **Correct Answer:** Option B
   **Difficulty:** Medium
   **Explanation:** FFR is the ratio of pressure distal to a stenosis compared to the aortic root pressure during maximal hyperemia. An FFR value ≤ 0.80 is the standard threshold for determining that a coronary stenosis is hemodynamically significant and likely causing ischemia.
## Q23
**Question:** The "Tirofiban" drug used in the cath lab acts as a:
 * **Option A:** Beta-blocker
 * **Option B:** Glycoprotein IIb/IIIa inhibitor (antiplatelet)
 * **Option C:** Calcium channel blocker
 * **Option D:** ACE inhibitor
   **Correct Answer:** Option B
   **Difficulty:** Medium
   **Explanation:** Tirofiban is a potent intravenous antiplatelet agent that works by blocking the GP IIb/IIIa receptor on the surface of platelets, preventing them from aggregating.
## Q24
**Question:** What is the normal range of the Pulmonary Artery (PA) systolic pressure?
 * **Option A:** 15-30 mmHg
 * **Option B:** 50-80 mmHg
 * **Option C:** 100-120 mmHg
 * **Option D:** 5-10 mmHg
   **Correct Answer:** Option A
   **Difficulty:** Medium
   **Explanation:** The normal pressure in the pulmonary artery is much lower than systemic pressure, with typical systolic values ranging from 15 to 30 mmHg.
## Q25
**Question:** Which of the following is a symptom of cardiac tamponade?
 * **Option A:** Pulsus paradoxus
 * **Option B:** Sudden increase in blood pressure
 * **Option C:** Bradycardia
 * **Option D:** Warm extremities
   **Correct Answer:** Option A
   **Difficulty:** Medium
   **Explanation:** Pulsus paradoxus—a significant drop in systolic blood pressure during inspiration—is a hallmark clinical finding in cardiac tamponade due to impaired right-to-left heart filling during the respiratory cycle.
