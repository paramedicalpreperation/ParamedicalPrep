## Q1
Question: When performing a venipuncture bright red blood spurts into the tube. This means:
Option A: An arterial puncture
Option B: High hemoglobin
Option C: High blood pressure
Option D: High blood pH
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Arterial blood is rich in oxygen, giving it a bright red color. Furthermore, arteries are under higher pressure than veins, which causes the blood to spurt rhythmically into the collection tube rather than flowing steadily, indicating an accidental arterial puncture.
