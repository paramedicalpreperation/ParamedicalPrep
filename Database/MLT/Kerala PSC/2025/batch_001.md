## Q1
Question: Which of the following anticoagulants is used for coagulation studies?
Option A: Trisodium citrate
Option B: Heparin
Option C: EDTA
Option D: Double oxalate
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Trisodium citrate (at a 3.2% concentration) is the standard anticoagulant for coagulation tests (such as PT and APTT) because it reversibly binds calcium, allowing the coagulation cascade to be re-initiated in the lab by adding calcium back to the plasma.
## Q2
Question: Iron deficiency anemia is characterized by all, EXCEPT:
Option A: MCV < 80 fl
Option B: MCH < 25 pg
Option C: MCHC < 27 g/dl
Option D: RDW < 13%
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: In iron deficiency anemia (IDA), the Red Cell Distribution Width (RDW) is typically increased (usually > 15%), indicating significant anisocytosis (variation in red blood cell size). MCV, MCH, and MCHC are typically decreased (microcytic, hypochromic).

Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: The Periodic acid-Schiff (PAS) stain typically shows characteristic coarse granular or "block-like" positivity in the cytoplasm of lymphoblasts in Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL).
## Q4
Question: Which of the following diluting fluids is used for absolute eosinophil count?
Option A: Hayem's
Option B: Turk's
Option C: Dunger's
Option D: Rees-Ecker
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Dunger's fluid, along with Pilot's fluid, is specifically used for absolute eosinophil counts. It contains eosin (to stain the eosinophils) and acetone/water to lyse the red blood cells and other leukocytes.
## Q5
Question: In peripheral smear staining by Leishman stain, undiluted stain is added first and then water is added only after 2 minutes. During this initial 2 minutes, there is:
Option A: Staining of smear
Option B: Settling of stain particles
Option C: Settling of cells
Option D: Fixation of smear
Correct Answer: Option D

Explanation: Leishman stain contains absolute methanol. During the initial undiluted phase, this methanol acts as a fixative, preserving the morphology of the blood cells before the addition of water/buffer initiates the actual dissociation and staining process.

## Q18
Question: Read the following statements about Barr Body and select the correct response:
i) Barr bodies are the inactive X chromosome in females
ii) Barr bodies are seen as well-defined body which stains intensely with nuclear dyes inside the nucleus at the edge.
iii) Buccal smears are used for demonstration of Barr body
iv) People with XXY chromosomal pattern may have two Barr bodies
v) Crystal violet stain can be used to demonstrate Barr body
vi) Barr bodies are seen inside the nucleus of all cells in females
Option A: i, ii & v are correct
Option B: ii, iii & iv are correct
Option C: iv, v & vi are correct
Option D: iii, iv & v are correct
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Statement (iv) is false because XXY individuals have only one Barr body (Number of Barr bodies = X chromosomes - 1). Statement (vi) is false because Barr bodies are not visible in *all* cells, only a proportion of somatic cells. Statements i, ii, and v are true.
