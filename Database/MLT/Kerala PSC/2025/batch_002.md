## Q51
Question: The following is an example of Dermatophyte:
Option A: Rhizopus
Option B: Sporothrix schenckii
Option C: Epidermophyton species
Option D: Histoplasma capsulatum
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Dermatophytes are a group of fungi that require keratin for growth and cause superficial infections of the skin, hair, and nails. *Epidermophyton*, *Microsporum*, and *Trichophyton* are the three main genera of dermatophytes.
## Q52
Question: The percentage of KOH used for skin scrapings and plucked hair samples:
Option A: 20%
Option B: 40%
Option C: 30%
Option D: 10%
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: A 10% Potassium Hydroxide (KOH) solution is routinely used for clearing skin scrapings and hair samples to dissolve keratin and make fungal elements visible, while 20% is typically reserved for thicker nail clippings.
## Q53
Question: Which of the following is the culture technique used to study the intact morphology of fungus?
Option A: Teased mount
Option B: Slide culture
Option C: India ink
Option D: Gram stain
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The slide culture technique allows fungi to grow directly on a microscope slide. This preserves the delicate arrangement of conidia and conidiophores exactly as they grow, which is often disrupted in a teased mount.
## Q54
Question: The fungus which is not cultivable:
Option A: Mucor
Option B: Pneumocystis jirovecii
Option C: Rhizopus
Option D: Cryptococcus neoformans
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: *Pneumocystis jirovecii* is an atypical fungus that cannot be grown on standard routine fungal culture media. Diagnosis relies on microscopic visualization using special stains (like silver stain) or PCR.
## Q55
Question: Negri bodies are the characteristic of:
Option A: Herpes simplex virus infection
Option B: Polio
Option C: Influenza
Option D: Rabies
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Negri bodies are pathognomonic eosinophilic cytoplasmic inclusion bodies found in the neurons of individuals or animals infected with the rabies virus.
## Q56
Question: All of the following are enveloped viruses, EXCEPT:
Option A: Human Immunodeficiency Virus
Option B: Hepatitis B Virus
Option C: Polio Virus
Option D: Rabies Virus
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The Poliovirus (a member of the Picornaviridae family) is a small, non-enveloped (naked) single-stranded RNA virus. HIV, HBV, and Rabies virus all possess lipid envelopes.
## Q57
Question: Which among the following is a continuous cell line?
Option A: HeLa cell line
Option B: Monkey kidney cell line
Option C: Human amnion cell line
Option D: Chick embryo cell line
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: HeLa is an immortal, continuous cell line derived from cervical cancer cells. The other options are examples of primary or semi-continuous (diploid) cell cultures that have a limited lifespan in vitro.
## Q58
Question: Primary viral infection may be diagnosed serologically by detection of:
Option A: Virus specific IgA
Option B: Virus specific IgM
Option C: Virus specific IgG
Option D: Virus specific IgD
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: IgM is the first immunoglobulin class produced in response to a primary infection. Its presence in serum indicates a recent or acute viral infection.
## Q59
Question: Ideal temperature for storage of specimens for less than 5 days for viral diagnosis:
Option A: 4°C
Option B: 25°C
Option C: 37°C
Option D: None of the above
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: If processing is delayed for a short period (up to 3-5 days), clinical specimens for viral isolation should be refrigerated at 4°C to preserve viral viability. Freezing at -20°C can actually destroy many enveloped viruses.
## Q60
Question: Rota virus can be detected in stool by:
Option A: Detection of antigen by ELISA
Option B: Electron microscopy
Option C: Detection of viral RNA by PCR
Option D: All of the above
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Rotavirus in stool specimens can be diagnosed using any of these methods: rapid antigen detection (ELISA/immunochromatography), visualizing the wheel-like particles under electron microscopy, or molecular detection via RT-PCR.
## Q61
Question: Which of the following statements is/are correct about new glassware?
(i) It is alkaline in nature and should be soaked in dilute acid before use.
(ii) It is acidic in nature and should be soaked in dilute alkali before use.
(iii) It should be soaked in 5% hypochlorite solution before use.
Option A: Only ii
Option B: Only i
Option C: Both i & iii
Option D: Both ii & iii
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: New laboratory glassware often has a slightly alkaline surface due to free sodium silicates left over from the manufacturing process. It should be neutralized by soaking in a dilute acid (like 1-2% HCl) before washing and using.
## Q62
Question: Phenol belongs to which category of lab chemical?
Option A: Flammable
Option B: Toxic
Option C: Explosive
Option D: Corrosive
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Phenol is highly corrosive and can cause severe chemical burns to the skin and mucous membranes upon contact, rapidly destroying tissue.
## Q63
Question: What is the term for the mixture that remains in the distillation flask after the distillation of water?
Option A: Distillate
Option B: Residue
Option C: Condensate
Option D: Azeotrope
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The pure liquid that vaporizes and condenses into the collection vessel is the distillate. The impurities and remaining liquid left behind in the boiling flask are called the residue.
## Q64
Question: Which of the following is a derived SI unit?
Option A: Meter (m)
Option B: Kilogram (kg)
Option C: Second (s)
Option D: Pascal (Pa)
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Meter, kilogram, and second are base SI units. The Pascal (Pa) is a derived SI unit of pressure, equivalent to one newton per square meter (N/m^2).
## Q65
Question: What is the formula to calculate Molarity?
Option A: Moles of solute / Liters of solution
Option B: Moles of solute / Kilograms of solvent
Option C: Grams of solute / Liters of solution
Option D: Moles of solvent / Liters of solution
Correct Answer: Option A

Explanation: Molarity (M) is a measure of concentration defined as the number of moles of a solute dissolved per liter of the total solution.
## Q66
Question: For the estimation of urine steroid, which specimen is more preferable?
Option A: Random sample
Option B: Early morning sample
Option C: 24 hr urine sample
Option D: All of the above
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Steroid hormones (like cortisol) exhibit significant diurnal variation (their levels fluctuate throughout the day). A 24-hour urine collection provides an accurate average of total daily hormone production.
## Q67
Question: What is the key component of chromic acid cleaning solution?

## Q68
Question: What is the mechanism of action of heparin as an anticoagulant?
Option A: Inhibiting thrombin directly
Option B: Activating fibrinolysis
Option C: Enhancing antithrombin III activity
Option D: Block platelet aggregation
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Heparin acts as an indirect anticoagulant. It binds to and dramatically accelerates the activity of antithrombin III, which in turn rapidly inactivates thrombin (Factor IIa) and Factor Xa.
## Q69
Question: Which is the ideal urine preservative for bacterial culture?
Option A: Conc. HCl
Option B: Toluene
Option C: Formalin
Option D: Boric acid
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Boric acid is the preservative of choice for urine bacterial cultures because it is bacteriostatic; it prevents the overgrowth of bacteria without killing the existing pathogens if processing is delayed.
## Q70
Question: Which of the following statements is/are correct?
(i) Refrigeration of blood specimen leads to false high potassium level.
(ii) Glycolysis can occur if fluoride–oxalate anticoagulant is not used.
(iii) Haemolysed sample can be used to prepare a pooled sample.
Option A: Only ii & iii
Option B: Only i & ii
Option C: Only i & iii
Option D: All of the above
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Refrigerating whole blood inhibits the Na+/K+ pump, allowing intracellular potassium to leak out and falsely elevate serum potassium. Without an inhibitor like fluoride, RBCs will continue glycolysis, lowering glucose levels. Hemolyzed samples are invalid for most pooling/QC as they release intracellular contents.
## Q71
Question: For HbA1C estimation, blood should be collected in:
Option A: Green colour coded vacutainer

Option C: Black colour coded vacutainer
Option D: Purple colour coded vacutainer
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Glycated hemoglobin (HbA1c) testing requires whole blood, which is routinely collected in a purple (lavender) top vacutainer containing EDTA.
## Q72
Question: Which of the following isotope is used to detect lifespan of RBC?
Option A: Technetium
Option B: Iodine
Option C: Phosphorous
Option D: Chromium
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Radioactive Chromium-51 (Cr-51) binds to the beta chains of hemoglobin and is the standard isotope used to label RBCs to measure their survival time (lifespan) and red cell mass.
## Q73
Question: Metabolic acidosis is characterised by:
Option A: Decreased bicarbonate
Option B: Increased bicarbonate
Option C: Decreased pCO2
Option D: Increased pCO2
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The primary hallmark of metabolic acidosis is a reduction in serum bicarbonate (HCO_3^-) levels, which drops the blood pH. (A secondary respiratory compensation may decrease pCO_2).
## Q74
Question: High anion gap acidosis is not seen in:
Option A: Diabetic ketoacidosis
Option B: Lactic acidosis
Option C: Renal failure
Option D: Renal tubular acidosis
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Renal tubular acidosis (RTA) is characterized by a normal anion gap (hyperchloremic) metabolic acidosis, due to the loss of bicarbonate which is offset by an increase in chloride. The other options classically cause high anion gap acidosis.
## Q75
Question: Pancreatic juice contains the following enzymes for lipid digestion, EXCEPT:
Option A: Hormone sensitive lipase
Option B: Phospholipase A2
Option C: Cholesterol esterase
Option D: Colipase
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Hormone-sensitive lipase is an intracellular enzyme found primarily in adipose tissue to mobilize stored triglycerides. The pancreas secretes pancreatic lipase, colipase, phospholipase A2, and cholesterol esterase into the intestine.
## Q76

## Q77

Explanation: Ketogenesis (the synthesis of ketone bodies) occurs exclusively in the mitochondria of hepatocytes in the liver during periods of fasting, starvation, or uncontrolled diabetes.
## Q78
Question: Which of the following molecules does not contain heme as a constituent?
Option A: Cytochrome
Option B: Peroxidase
Option C: Myoglobin
Option D: Ferroxidase
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Ferroxidase (also known as Ceruloplasmin) is a copper-containing enzyme involved in iron metabolism. Cytochromes, peroxidases, and myoglobin are all hemoproteins containing a heme group.
## Q79
Question: All of the following are considered as trace elements, EXCEPT:
Option A: Selenium
Option B: Iodine
Option C: Magnesium
Option D: Fluorine
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Magnesium is considered a major mineral (macronutrient) because it is required by the body in relatively large amounts. Selenium, iodine, and fluorine are trace elements (micronutrients) required in very small quantities.
## Q80
Question: The isoenzyme of creatine kinase elevated in myocardial infarction is:
Option A: CK-MB
Option B: CK-BB
Option C: CK-MM
Option D: All of the above
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Creatine kinase is composed of M (muscle) and B (brain) subunits. The heart muscle uniquely contains a significant fraction of the CK-MB isoenzyme, making its elevation highly specific for acute myocardial infarction.
## Q81
Question: Which among the following enzymes are activated on dephosphorylation process?
Option A: Glycogen phosphorylase
Option B: HMG – CoA reductase
Option C: Glycogen phosphorylase kinase
Option D: Citrate lyase
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: HMG-CoA reductase (the rate-limiting enzyme in cholesterol synthesis) and glycogen synthase are active in their dephosphorylated states (stimulated by insulin). Conversely, glycogen phosphorylase is active when phosphorylated.
## Q82
Question: Thyroid Stimulating Hormone (TSH) is measured after the administration of exogenous Thyrotropin Releasing Hormone (TRH) to assess the function of anterior pituitary gland. In this test, a delayed rise of TSH is seen in:
Option A: Primary hypothyroidism
Option B: Secondary hypothyroidism
Option C: Tertiary hypothyroidism
Option D: Hyperthyroidism

Explanation: In tertiary hypothyroidism (hypothalamic failure), the pituitary is healthy but lacks endogenous TRH. When exogenous TRH is given, the pituitary eventually responds, resulting in a delayed, prolonged rise in TSH.
## Q83
Question: Which is not a characteristic of genetic code?
Option A: Specific
Option B: Overlapping
Option C: Degenerate
Option D: Triplets
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The genetic code is non-overlapping, meaning each nucleotide belongs to only one codon and is read sequentially in triplets. It is also specific (unambiguous) and degenerate (redundant).
## Q84
Question: RNase H is used in:
Option A: Probe amplification
Option B: Transcription mediated amplification
Option C: Strand displacement amplification
Option D: Nucleic acid sequence based amplification

Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Nucleic Acid Sequence Based Amplification (NASBA) is an isothermal amplification technique that utilizes three enzymes: Reverse transcriptase, T7 RNA polymerase, and RNase H (which specifically degrades the RNA strand of an RNA-DNA hybrid).

## Q88
Question: Hypoacidity in stomach is seen in:
Option A: Zollinger-Ellison syndrome
Option B: Gastric carcinoma
Option C: Carcinoid tumour
Option D: Multiple endocrine neoplasia
Correct Answer: Option B

Explanation: Gastric carcinoma and pernicious anemia are classically associated with hypoacidity or achlorhydria (lack of stomach acid). In contrast, Zollinger-Ellison syndrome is characterized by massive acid hypersecretion.
## Q89
Question: Factors responsible for the formation of urinary stones are all, except:
Option A: Obstruction of urine flow
Option B: Urinary infection
Option C: Hypoparathyroidism
Option D: Hypervitaminosis D
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Hypoparathyroidism leads to low blood calcium levels (hypocalcemia) and decreased urinary calcium excretion, which reduces the risk of calcium stone formation. Hyperparathyroidism and hypervitaminosis D cause hypercalcemia and increase stone risk.
## Q90
Question: Increased mobility of molecules in electrophoresis is seen in all conditions, except:
Option A: High resistance of electric field
Option B: High voltage of electric field
Option C: High charge of molecules
Option D: Compact structure of molecules
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Electrophoretic mobility is directly proportional to voltage, the net charge of the molecule, and inversely proportional to molecular size/resistance. High resistance (or friction) slows down the mobility of the molecules.
## Q91
Question: In which type of chromatography techniques, the larger molecules will come out first, while the smaller molecules are retained in the column?
Option A: Adsorption chromatography
Option B: Gel filtration chromatography
Option C: Thin layer chromatography
Option D: Paper chromatography
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: In gel filtration (size-exclusion) chromatography, the column is packed with porous beads. Small molecules enter the pores and are delayed, while larger molecules bypass the pores and elute first.

## Q95

## Q96
Question: _____ is the fraction of those without the disease that the test correctly predicts.

Option B: Specificity
Option C: Sensitivity

Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Specificity is the ability of a test to correctly identify individuals who do *not* have the disease (True Negative Rate). Sensitivity identifies those *with* the disease.
