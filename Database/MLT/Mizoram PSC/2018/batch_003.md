## Q51
Question: Primary amyloid often affects:
Option A: Muscle, heart, skin and tongue
Option B: Liver, spleen, kidney and adrenal glands
Option C: Bone, cartilage and fat
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Primary amyloidosis (AL amyloidosis) typically involves mesenchymal tissues such as the heart, skin, tongue, and muscles, whereas secondary amyloidosis more commonly affects the liver, spleen, and kidneys.
## Q52
Question: In Highman’s Congo red technique, Amyloid stained:
Option A: Red
Option B: Pinkish
Option C: Red-purple
Option D: Pinkish-red
Correct Answer: D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Congo red is the standard stain for amyloid, which stains amyloid deposits a pinkish-red color, and exhibits characteristic apple-green birefringence under polarized light.
## Q53
Question: In H&E stain what percentage of yellow eosin is used:
Option A: 1.5 %
Option B: 1 %
Option C: 2%
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: In standard Hematoxylin and Eosin (H&E) staining protocols, a 1% aqueous or alcoholic solution of Eosin is commonly used as the counterstain.
## Q54
Question: Alum haematoxylene includes the following, except:
Option A: Harris haematoxylene
Option B: Delafield’s hematoxylene
Option C: Cole hematoxylene
Option D: Heidenhain’s hematoxylene
Correct Answer: D
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Heidenhain’s hematoxylin is an iron-hematoxylin stain, whereas Harris, Delafield’s, and Cole’s are all varieties of alum hematoxylin.
## Q55
Question: Refractive Index of the mounting media should be:
Option A: 1.53 and 1.54
Option B: 1.52 and 1.56
Option C: 1.54 and 1.58
Option D: All of these
Correct Answer: D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: An ideal mounting medium should have a refractive index close to that of fixed, dehydrated tissue (approximately 1.53 to 1.55) to ensure transparency and clarity of the microscopic image.
## Q56
Question: Combined (fast) smears mean:
Option A: Lateral vaginal wall scraping and vaginal pool smear
Option B: Combination of vaginal pool and cervical scraping
Option C: Cervical scraping and lateral vaginal wall scraping
Option D: All of these
Correct Answer: D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Combined or "fast" smears are multi-site cytologic preparations used to increase the diagnostic sensitivity for detecting cervical or vaginal pathology.
## Q57
Question: For determination of malignant or benign in a cell - the following are important, except:
Option A: Abnormal mitosis
Option B: Increased N:C ratio
Option C: Multinucleation
Option D: Anisonucleosis
Correct Answer: C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: While multinucleation can be seen in malignant cells, it is also frequently seen in benign reactive processes (e.g., giant cells), making it a less specific indicator of malignancy compared to abnormal mitosis or high N:C ratio.
## Q58
Question: Navicular Cells (Pregnancy) are, except:
Option A: Vesicular nucleus
Option B: Oval
Option C: Eccentric nucleus
Option D: Thick borders
Correct Answer: A
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Navicular cells are glycogen-rich intermediate squamous cells found in pregnancy smears. They typically have a "folded" appearance and a dense, eccentric nucleus, not a vesicular one.
## Q59
Question: Nuclear changes due to radiation includes the following, except:
Option A: Lysis
Option B: Karyorrhexis
Option C: Faint blue cytoplasm
Option D: Crenation of nuclear membrane
Correct Answer: C
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Radiation causes various nuclear damages (lysis, fragmentation, membrane changes), but the cytoplasmic staining quality (like "faint blue cytoplasm") is not a primary nuclear change associated with radiation.
## Q60
Question: Body fluid for Cytological examination of high protein content should not be refrigerated more than:
Option A: 48 hours
Option B: 36 hours
Option C: 72 hours
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: To prevent cellular degeneration and protein precipitation, samples with high protein content should be processed within 48 hours; refrigeration only slows, does not stop, degradation.
## Q61
Question: Preservation of choice for sputum, Bronchial aspirate, bronchial washing is:
Option A: 95% Ethanol
Option B: 70% Ethanol
Option C: 50% Ethanol
Option D: 85% Ethanol
Correct Answer: A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: 95% Ethanol is the gold standard fixative for gynecological and non-gynecological cytology samples (like sputum) to preserve detailed nuclear and cytoplasmic architecture.
## Q62
Question: For routine transcutaneous FNAC of Palpable masses the choice of the needle is:
Option A: 24 gauge of 25 mm length
Option B: 25 gauge of 39 mm length
Option C: 21 gauge of 38 mm length
Option D: Both (a) and (b)
Correct Answer: D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Fine Needle Aspiration Cytology (FNAC) of palpable masses typically uses needles ranging from 21G to 25G depending on the site and characteristics of the mass.
## Q63
Question: The common fixative for cell block preparation are, except:
Option A: Bouin’s fixative
Option B: Picric Acid fixative
Option C: 10% Formaline
Option D: Glutaraldehyde
Correct Answer: B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Formalin and Bouin’s are standard fixatives for tissue; Glutaraldehyde is used for electron microscopy. Picric acid is a component of Bouin's but not a standard standalone fixative for cell blocks.
## Q64
Question: Effect of alcohol fixation are, except:
Option A: Enlarges the nucleus
Option B: Denature and precipitate protein
Option C: Coarsens cell structures
Option D: Sharpens nuclear chromatin pattern
Correct Answer: A
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Alcohol fixation causes cellular shrinkage (which can lead to artifactual changes), not nuclear enlargement.
## Q65
Question: OG6 in Pap’s stain is for:
Option A: Stain metabolically active cells
Option B: Stain nucleoli
Option C: Cytoplasmic counter stain
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: OG6 (Orange G) is a cytoplasmic counterstain in the Papanicolaou method, primarily staining mature (superficial) squamous cells.
