# MCQ DATABASE
Course: Laboratory Technician
Exam Name: Mizoram Institute of Medical Education and Research (MIMER)
Year: 2018
## Q1
Question: The microscopic features of chronic inflammatory cells include the following, except:
Option A: Monocytes
Option B: Macrophage
Option C: Lymphocytes
Option D: Eosinophils
Correct Answer: D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Chronic inflammation is characterized primarily by mononuclear cells, including lymphocytes, plasma cells, and macrophages. Eosinophils are typically associated with allergic reactions or parasitic infections, not chronic inflammation.
## Q2
Question: Phagocytosis involves the following, except:
Option A: Recognition and attachment
Option B: Release of histamine
Option C: Engulfment
Option D: Killing or degradation
Correct Answer: B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Phagocytosis is the process of engulfing and destroying pathogens. The release of histamine is a function of mast cells or basophils involved in vascular permeability during inflammation, not phagocytosis itself.
## Q3
Question: The following are the chemical mediators of inflammation, except:
Option A: Monocytes
Option B: Histamine
Option C: Chemokines
Option D: Complements
Correct Answer: A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Monocytes are cells involved in the inflammatory response, whereas histamine, chemokines, and complements are chemical mediators (signaling molecules).
## Q4
Question: Hyperplasia means:
Option A: Increase in the size of cells that results in an increase in the size of the organs.
Option B: Increase in the number of cells in an organ or tissue in response to stimulus.
Option C: Increase in the size and number of cells to increase the size of the organs.
Option D: None of these.
Correct Answer: B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Hyperplasia is specifically defined as an increase in the number of cells, while hypertrophy refers to an increase in the size of cells.
## Q5
Question: Nuclear changes of necrosis include the following, except:
Option A: Karyolysis
Option B: Pyknosis
Option C: Fragmentation of the cell wall
Option D: Karyorrhexis
Correct Answer: C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Nuclear changes in necrosis include pyknosis, karyorrhexis, and karyolysis. "Fragmentation of the cell wall" is not a recognized nuclear change.
## Q6
Question: The cells of the immune system are, except:
Option A: T lymphocytes and B lymphocytes
Option B: Monocytes
Option C: Natural killer cells
Option D: Macrophages
Correct Answer: B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: While monocytes circulate in the blood and can become macrophages, they are generally classified as part of the innate immune system's myeloid lineage, whereas lymphocytes and NK cells are the primary lymphoid immune cells.
## Q7
Question: Lack of differentiation, both morphologically and functionally to the corresponding normal parenchymal cells is termed as:
Option A: Metaplasia
Option B: Anaplasia
Option C: Pleomorphism
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Anaplasia refers to the reversal of differentiation to a more primitive, undifferentiated state, which is a hallmark of malignant tumors.
## Q8
Question: The following are the characteristics of malignant tumours, except:
Option A: Less differentiated
Option B: Retain functions of their cell of origin
Option C: Local invasiveness
Option D: Distant spread
Correct Answer: B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Malignant tumors typically lose the specialized functions of their cells of origin as they become less differentiated.
## Q9
Question: Acid burns in laboratory accidents occur with the following acids, except:
Option A: Nitric Acid
Option B: Sulphuric Acid
Option C: Hydrochloric Acid
Option D: Acetic Acid
Correct Answer: D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: While acetic acid can be corrosive in high concentrations, nitric, sulphuric, and hydrochloric acids are strong mineral acids and are the most common causes of severe chemical burns in laboratory settings.
## Q10
Question: Greenish colouration of urine is due to:
Option A: Jaundice
Option B: Haemolysis
Option C: Phenol poisoning
Option D: Both (a) and (c)
Correct Answer: D
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Jaundice (biliverdin) and phenol poisoning are well-documented causes of green-colored urine.
## Q11
Question: High specific gravity of urine is due to:
Option A: Chronic nephritis
Option B: Glycosuria
Option C: Albuminuria
Option D: Acute Nephritis
Correct Answer: B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Glycosuria (glucose in urine) increases the concentration of solutes, thereby raising the specific gravity of the urine.
## Q12
Question: The normal pH of stool is:
Option A: Slightly acid and slightly alkaline
Option B: Neutral
Option C: Neutral or weakly alkaline
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The normal pH of stool in healthy adults is typically neutral to weakly alkaline, depending on dietary fiber intake and bacterial fermentation.
## Q13
Question: Spermatozoa comprises about:
Option A: 6% of semen volume
Option B: 7% of semen volume
Option C: 10% of semen volume
Option D: 5% of semen volume
Correct Answer: D
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Spermatozoa make up a very small fraction of the total semen volume, usually estimated at around 5%.
## Q14
Question: In the first few weeks of gestation, the main site of haemopoiesis is:
Option A: Spleen
Option B: Lymph node
Option C: Yolk sac
Option D: Bone marrow
Correct Answer: C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The yolk sac is the primary site of hematopoiesis during the early embryonic stage (mesoblastic phase).
## Q15
Question: When EDTA is used as an anticoagulant, the following are true, except:
Option A: Leucocyte morphology is not well preserved
Option B: PCV is significantly reduced
Option C: MCHC is proportionately increased
Option D: Platelets swell and disintegrate
Correct Answer: A
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: EDTA is the anticoagulant of choice for hematology precisely because it preserves leucocyte and platelet morphology for up to 24 hours.
## Q16
Question: Falsely high count of TLC (Total Leucocyte Count) can occur in:
Option A: Delay in counting
Option B: Dilution fluid not taken up to the requisite mark
Option C: Blood not drawn up to the requisite mark
Option D: Dilution fluid taken in excess of the requisite mark
Correct Answer: C
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: If blood is drawn below the requisite mark during dilution, the concentration ratio is skewed, potentially leading to inaccurate high counts in the hemocytometer.
## Q17
Question: Microcytosis of RBCs is found in, except:
Option A: Thalassaemia
Option B: Folic acid deficiency
Option C: Haemoglobinopathies
Option D: Spherocytosis
Correct Answer: B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Folic acid deficiency typically causes macrocytic (large RBCs) anemia, whereas the others are typically microcytic or normocytic.
## Q18
Question: Haemoglobin contains about:
Option A: 1/2 of body iron
Option B: 1/5 of body iron
Option C: 2/3 of body iron
Option D: 1/4 of body iron
Correct Answer: C
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Approximately 65-70% (roughly two-thirds) of the total body iron is incorporated into hemoglobin.
## Q19
Question: Iron is absorbed in:
Option A: Ferric form
Option B: Ferrous form
Option C: Ferritin form
Option D: Haemosiderin form
Correct Answer: B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Dietary iron must be reduced from the ferric (Fe^{3+}) to the ferrous (Fe^{2+}) state to be absorbed by the intestinal mucosa.
## Q20
Question: In Giemsa’s stain, the required amount of Giemsa’s powder is:
Option A: 0.2 gm
Option B: 0.3 gm
Option C: 0.4 gm
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Standard Giemsa stain preparation usually requires 0.3g of Giemsa powder dissolved in glycerol and methanol.
## Q21
Question: Evidence of haemolysis are, except:
Option A: Increased plasma LDH
Option B: Increased urinary urobilinogen
Option C: Hyperbilirubinaemia
Option D: Basophilic stippling
Correct Answer: D
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Basophilic stippling is a feature of lead poisoning or dyserythropoiesis, not a direct sign of hemolysis like the other options.
## Q22
Question: Laboratory features of SLE include, except:
Option A: Platelets decreased
Option B: Haemoglobin and PCV decreased
Option C: Reticulocytes increased
Option D: ANA positive
Correct Answer: C
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: SLE often presents with anemia and thrombocytopenia, but a significant increase in reticulocytes (suggesting brisk hemolysis) is not a defining feature of the condition itself.
## Q23
Question: Typical blood picture of Acute Leukaemia are, except:
Option A: High MCH
Option B: Anaemia
Option C: Thrombocytopaenia
Option D: Moderate or marked increased blasts cells
Correct Answer: A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Acute leukemia is characterized by anemia, thrombocytopenia, and an abundance of blast cells; MCH (Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin) is typically normal.
## Q24
Question: In Alpha Thalassaemia there is:
Option A: Deletion of four alpha chain
Option B: Free Gamma chains form tetrads, producing haemoglobin
Option C: B-chain production is absent or diminished
Option D: Both (a) and (b)
Correct Answer: D
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Alpha thalassemia involves the deletion of alpha-globin genes; the resulting excess gamma chains (in newborns) form Hemoglobin Barts (tetrads).
## Q25
Question: Indirect Coomb’s test is used to detect:
Option A: Antibody identification
Option B: To detect incomplete antibodies during pregnancy
Option C: To differentiate between acquired and congenital haemolytic anaemias
Option D: Both (a) and (b)
Correct Answer: D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The Indirect Antiglobulin Test (IAT) is used to identify unexpected antibodies in serum and is a standard procedure in prenatal testing.
## Q26
Question: Indication of autologous transfusion includes the following, except:
Option A: Requirement of rare blood types
Option B: Prevention of alloimmunization
Option C: Religious belief
Option D: In an accidental injury
Correct Answer: D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Autologous transfusion requires pre-donation, which is impossible in emergency situations like an accidental injury.
## Q27
Question: In G6PD deficiency, the following are true, except:
Option A: Deficiency in apoprotein
Option B: Increased reticulocyte counts
Option C: Polychromasia
Option D: Normochromic, normocytic anaemia
Correct Answer: A
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: G6PD deficiency is an enzyme deficiency, not a deficiency of an apoprotein.
## Q28
Question: The richest sources of Vitamin B12 are, except:
Option A: Vegetables
Option B: Kidneys
Option C: Liver
Option D: Heart
Correct Answer: A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Vitamin B12 is found almost exclusively in animal products; plant-based foods are extremely poor sources.
## Q29
Question: Pancytopaenia is the simultaneous presence of the following, except:
Option A: Anaemia
Option B: (a) and (c) only
Option C: Leucopaenia
Option D: Thrombocytopaenia
Correct Answer: B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Pancytopenia is the presence of all three (anemia, leucopenia, and thrombocytopenia). Option B is grammatically incorrect in the context of the list.
## Q30
Question: The structural formula of Hb-A2 is:
Option A: Alpha-2, Beta-2
Option B: Alpha-2, Gamma-2
Option C: Alpha-2, Delta-2
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Hemoglobin A2 is composed of two alpha and two delta globin chains (\alpha_2\delta_2).
