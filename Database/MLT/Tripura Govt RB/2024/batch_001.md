## Q1
Question: In screening, how is the diagnostic specificity of a test defined?
Option A: All affected individuals identified, divided by total of positive results.
Option B: All affected individuals identified, divided by total of those with condition.
Option C: All correct results, divided by total number of subjects studied.
Option D: All healthy individuals identified, divided by total number unaffected.
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Specificity is the ability of a test to correctly identify healthy individuals (true negatives), calculated as true negatives divided by the total number of unaffected subjects.

## Q2
Question: A 3-year-old boy was investigated for short stature. What is the most likely cause of the low ALP?
Option A: EDTA contamination
Option B: Hypophosphatasia
Option C: Magnesium deficiency
Option D: Zinc deficiency
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: ALP activity requires zinc and magnesium as cofactors. EDTA is a chelating agent that binds these metals, leading to falsely low ALP results when samples are contaminated with it.

## Q4
Question: What is the faecal osmotic gap calculated from the following: Osmolality 280 mOsm/Kg, Faecal sodium 20 mmol/L, Faecal potassium 65 mmol/L?
Option A: 40 mOsm/kg
Option B: 110 mOsm/kg
Option C: 130 mOsm/kg
Option D: 175 mOsm/kg
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Osmotic gap = Osmolality - 2 x (Na + K). Gap = 280 - 2 x (20 + 65) = 280 - 170 = 110 mOsm/kg.

Option A: 300-410
Option B: 400-510
Option C: 500-610

## Q25
Question: A baby had a positive newborn screening for cystic fibrosis. A sweat test performed at three weeks old showed a sweat chloride of 37 mmol/L. What interpretative comment is added?
Option A: Result not consistent with cystic fibrosis
Option B: Result consistent with cystic fibrosis
Option C: Result suggestive but not diagnostic
Option D: Insufficient sweat volume; repeat test required
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Guidelines state that a sweat chloride test requires a minimum volume of 15 mg (or 75 mg for filter paper) to be considered reliable. With only 14 mg collected, the sample volume is insufficient for a valid diagnostic result.
