## Q26
Question: The condition required for autoclaving is:
Option A: 121°C temp and 15 lbs pressure for 15 min
Option B: 121°C temp and 20 lbs pressure for 30 min
Option C: 150°C temp for 1 hr
Option D: 130°C temp for 2 hr
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Autoclaving is a sterilization method that typically uses saturated steam at 121°C and 15 psi (lbs) of pressure for a duration of 15 to 20 minutes.
## Q27
Question: Blood agar medium is:
Option A: Enrichment medium
Option B: Enriched medium
Option C: Selective medium
Option D: Differential medium
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Blood agar is an enriched medium containing 5-10% blood, which supports the growth of fastidious organisms and also acts as a differential medium to detect hemolysis.
## Q28
Question: Glassware are sterilized by:
Option A: Autoclaving
Option B: Hot air oven
Option C: Incineration
Option D: Inspissation
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Dry heat sterilization using a hot air oven is the standard and preferred method for sterilizing laboratory glassware as it avoids the moisture and potential breakage associated with autoclaving.
## Q29
Question: On MacConkey's medium, Escherichia coli forms:
Option A: Colourless colonies
Option B: Greenish pigmentation
Option C: Pink coloured colonies
Option D: Medusa head appearance
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: MacConkey's medium is a selective and differential medium. E. coli is a lactose fermenter and produces acid, which turns the neutral red indicator in the medium pink.
## Q30
Question: Which of the following tests is used to differentiate Staphylococcus aureus from other Staphylococcus spp.?
Option A: Coagulase Test
Option B: Catalase Test
Option C: Urease Test
Option D: Oxidase Test
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The coagulase test is the definitive biochemical test used to distinguish S. aureus (which produces the coagulase enzyme) from coagulase-negative staphylococci.
## Q31
Question: Cerebral malaria is caused by:
Option A: Plasmodium vivax
Option B: Plasmodium ovale
Option C: Plasmodium malariae
Option D: Plasmodium falciparum
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Plasmodium falciparum is the most virulent species and is specifically associated with severe complications like cerebral malaria due to sequestration of infected red blood cells in the brain capillaries.
## Q32
Question: Mycobacterium tuberculosis resists decolourization from:
Option A: 20% sulphuric acid
Option B: 5% sulphuric acid
Option C: 1% sulphuric acid
Option D: 3% sulphuric acid
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: The acid-fast property of M. tuberculosis is determined by its waxy cell wall (mycolic acids), which remains stained with carbol fuchsin even after treatment with strong 20% sulphuric acid.
## Q33
Question: Which part of the light microscope converges the light beam so that it passes through the specimen?
Option A: Objective lens
Option B: Mechanical stage
Option C: Condenser
Option D: Iris diaphragm
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The condenser lens gathers light from the illuminator and focuses (converges) it into a cone of light that passes through the specimen, ensuring maximum illumination.
## Q34
Question: The solidifying agent of a culture media is:
Option A: Peptone
Option B: Meat extract
Option C: Sodium chloride
Option D: Agar
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Agar is a complex polysaccharide derived from seaweed, used globally as the standard solidifying agent because it remains stable at various incubation temperatures.
## Q35
Question: Paraffin oil can be sterilized by using:
Option A: Autoclave
Option B: Hot air oven
Option C: Vaccine bath
Option D: Pasteurization
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Paraffin oil is a non-aqueous substance that cannot be sterilized by steam (autoclaving). It requires dry heat sterilization in a hot air oven for extended periods.
## Q36
Question: RPR and VDRL tests are done for the diagnosis of:
Option A: AIDS
Option B: Hepatitis
Option C: Syphilis
Option D: Malaria
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: RPR (Rapid Plasma Reagin) and VDRL (Venereal Disease Research Laboratory) are non-treponemal screening tests used to detect reaginic antibodies produced during a syphilis infection.
## Q37
Question: Salmonella typhi is the causative organism of:
Option A: Undulant fever
Option B: Remittent fever
Option C: Enteric fever
Option D: Dengue fever
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Salmonella typhi is the bacterium responsible for typhoid fever, which is clinically classified as an enteric fever.

Option A: Sand fly
Option B: Ticks
Option C: Aedes aegypti

## Q39
Question: Man is the intermediate host for:
Option A: Guinea Worm
Option B: Filaria
Option C: Malaria
Option D: Kala-azar
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: In the lifecycle of Plasmodium (malaria), the mosquito is the definitive host (where sexual reproduction occurs), and man is the intermediate host (where asexual reproduction occurs).
## Q40
Question: Which method is used for cultivation of viruses?
Option A: Incubation in embryonated eggs
Option B: Animal inoculation
Option C: Tissue culture
Option D: All the above
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Viruses are obligate intracellular parasites and cannot grow on cell-free media; they require living host systems like embryonated eggs, laboratory animals, or cultured living cells (tissue culture) for replication.
## Q41
Question: Identify the correct sequence of steps for the gram stain procedure:
Option A: Primary stain, counter stain, mordant, decolorizing
Option B: Primary stain, mordant, counter stain, decolorizing
Option C: Primary stain, decolorizing, counter stain, mordant
Option D: Primary stain, mordant, decolorizing, counter stain
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The standard sequence is Crystal violet (primary), Iodine (mordant), Alcohol/Acetone (decolorizer), and Safranin (counter stain).
## Q42
Question: Culture media of choice for culture of urine sample:
Option A: Blood agar
Option B: Chocolate agar
Option C: CLED agar
Option D: XLD agar
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: CLED (Cystine Lactose Electrolyte Deficient) agar is widely used for urine culture as it supports the growth of most urinary pathogens and inhibits the swarming of Proteus species.
## Q43
Question: Bacteria having flagella all over the body are called:
Option A: Monotrichous
Option B: Peritrichous
Option C: Lophotrichous
Option D: Amphitrichious
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Peritrichous bacteria have flagella distributed uniformly over the entire surface of the cell, as seen in E. coli.
## Q44
Question: Which of the following parasite has no cystic stage?
Option A: Giardia lamblia
Option B: Balantidium coli
Option C: Trichomonas vaginalis
Option D: Entamoeba histolytica
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Trichomonas vaginalis is a flagellated protozoan that exists only in the trophozoite form and does not produce a resistant cystic stage in its life cycle.
## Q45
Question: All the following are sporicidal except:
Option A: Glutaraldehyde
Option B: Alcohols
Option C: Ethylene oxide
Option D: Stabilized hydrogen peroxide
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Alcohols (like ethyl or isopropyl) are effective against vegetative bacteria, fungi, and some viruses, but they lack sporicidal activity, meaning they cannot kill bacterial spores.
## Q46
Question: All of the following characteristics are seen in the stools in amoebic dysentery except:
Option A: RBCs in clumps
Option B: Charcot-leyden crystals
Option C: Pyknotic bodies
Option D: Ghost cells
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Ghost cells are typically associated with malarial parasites in blood smears, not amoebic dysentery in stool samples.
## Q47
Question: Stoll's method is used for:
Option A: Determining the number of helminthic eggs in faeces
Option B: Demonstration of Cryptosporidium oocysts in faeces
Option C: Concentrating microfilariae in blood
Option D: Staining of lymph node smear for Leishmania donovani
Correct Answer: Option A

## Q48
Question: Which medium is the most ideal for antibiotic sensitivity testing of bacterial isolates?
Option A: Mueller-Hinton agar
Option B: Nutrient agar
Option C: Blood agar
Option D: Macconkey agar
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Mueller-Hinton agar is the standardized medium for the Kirby-Bauer disk diffusion susceptibility test because it allows for optimal growth and uniform diffusion of antibiotics.

## Q50
Question: Hand rub should not be used in which condition?
Option A: Before touching patient
Option B: After touching a patient
Option C: After touching patient's surrounding
Option D: Hands are visibly soiled
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Alcohol-based hand rubs are ineffective on hands that are visibly dirty, contaminated with organic matter, or soiled; soap and running water must be used in these cases.
