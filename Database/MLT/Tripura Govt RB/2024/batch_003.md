## Q50
Question: Stoll's method is used for:
Option A: Determining the number of helminthic eggs in faeces
Option B: Demonstration of Cryptosporidium oocysts in faeces
Option C: Concentrating microfilariae in blood
Option D: Staining of lymph node smear for Leishmania donovani
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Stoll's method is a dilution technique specifically used for the quantitative estimation of helminth eggs in a fecal sample.
## Q51
Question: Gloves should be segregated in which colour bags?
Option A: Yellow bags
Option B: Red bag
Option C: Blue bag
Option D: White/translucent bin
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Waste gloves are typically segregated into white/translucent puncture-proof containers for recycling purposes.
## Q52
Question: Indian ink is used to demonstrate:
Option A: Cell wall
Option B: Bacterial capsule
Option C: Bacterial flagella
Option D: Bacterial spore
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Indian ink is a negative staining technique where the ink particles are excluded by the bacterial capsule, creating a clear halo against a dark background.
## Q53
Question: Which of the following diseases can be transmitted by water and food?
Option A: Cholera
Option B: Poliomyelitis
Option C: Hepatitis A virus infection
Option D: All of the above
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Cholera, Polio, and Hepatitis A are classic examples of fecal-oral route diseases frequently transmitted through contaminated water or food supplies.
## Q54

## Q55
Question: Which HIV testing strategy is used for HIV Surveillance?
Option A: Strategy I
Option B: Strategy IIA
Option C: Strategy IIB
Option D: Strategy III

Explanation: HIV testing Strategy III is recommended for surveillance purposes as well as for the diagnosis of clinical cases, ensuring the highest specificity.
## Q56
Question: Which of the following is used as anticoagulant in estimating ESR by Westergren method?
Option A: Double oxalate
Option B: Heparin
Option C: 3.8% Trisodium citrate
Option D: EDTA
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The Westergren method requires 3.8% trisodium citrate as an anticoagulant, mixed with blood in a 1:4 ratio.
## Q57
Question: Pancytopenia means:
Option A: Reduction in RBC and WBC count
Option B: Reduction in WBC and platelet count
Option C: Reduction in RBC and platelet count
Option D: Reduction in RBC, WBC and Platelet count
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Pancytopenia is characterized by a simultaneous deficiency of all three major blood cell components: red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets.
## Q58
Question: Supravital stain is used for counting:
Option A: Sperm
Option B: Reticulocyte
Option C: Platelet
Option D: Yeast cell
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Supravital stains like New Methylene Blue or Brilliant Cresyl Blue are used to stain the residual RNA in reticulocytes for microscopic counting.
## Q59
Question: All of the following are used in preservation of urine sample except:
Option A: Refrigeration
Option B: Sulfa-drugs
Option C: Boric acid
Option D: Thymol
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Refrigeration, boric acid, and thymol are common urine preservatives; sulfa-drugs are medications and not used for urine preservation.
## Q60
Question: To estimate PCV blood has to be centrifuged at:
Option A: 2000 rpm for 1 hr
Option B: 1000 rpm for 15 mins
Option C: 5000 rpm for 30 mins
Option D: None of the above
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: In traditional macro-methods, centrifugation at 3000–5000 rpm for 30 minutes is often required to estimate Packed Cell Volume (PCV).
## Q61
Question: Site of collection of blood from newborn baby:
Option A: Heel of baby's foot
Option B: Ante-cubital fossa
Option C: Ear lobe
Option D: Finger prick
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The heel is the preferred site for capillary blood collection in newborns to avoid potential damage to the smaller veins of the arm or fingers.
## Q62
Question: Stains used in sputum microscopy:
Option A: Gram stain
Option B: Ziehl-Neelsen stain
Option C: Giemsa stain
Option D: All of the above
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Various stains are used in sputum microscopy depending on the suspected pathogen, including Gram stain, Ziehl-Neelsen, and Giemsa.
## Q63
Question: In microcytic hypochromic anemia, MCV is expected to be:
Option A: 80-98 fl
Option B: < 80 fl
Option C: 100-120 fl
Option D: > 120 fl
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Microcytic anemia is characterized by a Mean Corpuscular Volume (MCV) of less than 80 femtoliters (fl).
## Q64
Question: In a patient of polycythemia, for preparing peripheral blood smear what should be the angle of the spreader:
Option A: 90°
Option B: 30°
Option C: 45°
Option D: 60°
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: In patients with high hematocrit, blood is viscous, so the spreader angle should be increased (often 45-60°) to make a proper smear.
## Q65
Question: What is the depth of counting area in Improved Neubauer chamber?
Option A: 0.1 mm
Option B: 0.16 mm
Option C: 0.4 mm
Option D: 0.5 mm
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The depth of the chamber is calibrated to be exactly 0.1 mm between the cover slip and the ruled base of the counting grid.
## Q66
Question: Positive osmotic fragility test indicates which disorder:
Option A: CML
Option B: Sideroblastic anemia
Option C: Hereditary spherocytosis
Option D: G-6-PD deficiency
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Hereditary spherocytosis is characterized by abnormal RBC membrane permeability, which causes cells to burst at higher salt concentrations in an osmotic fragility test.
## Q67

Option A: a2b2
Option B: a2d2
Option C: a2g2
Option D: b4
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Normal adult hemoglobin (HbA) is composed of two alpha and two beta globin chains.
## Q68
Question: All of the following are the cytochemical stains used to identify blast cells in acute leukemia except:
Option A: MPO
Option B: Reticulin
Option C: Sudan black
Option D: PAS
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: MPO, Sudan Black B, and PAS are standard cytochemical stains for leukemia, whereas reticulin is a stain used to assess fibrosis in bone marrow biopsies.
## Q69
Question: Very low platelet count is usually associated with:
Option A: Prolonged CT
Option B: Both BT & CT prolonged
Option C: Prolonged BT
Option D: Prolonged APTT
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Platelets are essential for primary plug formation, so low platelet count (thrombocytopenia) results in a prolonged Bleeding Time (BT).

## Q71

Option B: A Ag
Option C: B Ag
Option D: h Ag
Correct Answer: Option D

## Q72
Question: Which of the following is WBC diluting fluid?

Option B: Turk's fluid
Option C: Dacie's fluid
Option D: Toluidine fluid

Explanation: Turk's fluid is used as a diluting fluid for counting white blood cells, as it lyses red blood cells while staining white blood cell nuclei.
## Q73
