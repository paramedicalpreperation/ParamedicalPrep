## Q36
Question: Tumor markers:
Option A: CA125
Option B: CA19.9
Option C: CEA
Option D: All the above
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: CA125 (ovarian), CA19.9 (pancreatic), and CEA (colorectal) are all well-established tumor markers used in clinical diagnostics to monitor and detect specific cancers.
## Q37
Question: What is PPE in laboratory?
Option A: Personal protective equipment
Option B: Pipette per equipment
Option C: Professional pipetter
Option D: None of them
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: PPE stands for Personal Protective Equipment, which includes items like gloves, lab coats, and safety goggles essential for protecting laboratory personnel from hazardous materials.
## Q38
Question: The liquid portion of blood remaining after a clot has formed is called:
Option A: Plasma
Option B: Serum
Option C: Tissue
Option D: Lymph
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Serum is the clear, yellowish fluid that remains after whole blood is allowed to clot. Unlike plasma, serum does not contain fibrinogen or other clotting factors.
## Q39
Question: Which test cannot be performed on a serum sample?
Option A: Iron
Option B: Vitamin B12
Option C: Total lipids
Option D: Clotting factors
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Clotting factors (like fibrinogen) are consumed during the blood coagulation process. Therefore, they are absent in serum and must be measured using a plasma sample (typically collected in a sodium citrate tube).
## Q40
Question: A suction device used to draw up liquids is a:
Option A: Flask
Option B: Cylinder
Option C: Beaker
Option D: Safety bulb
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: A safety bulb (or pipette bulb) is a rubber device used to safely create a vacuum to draw liquids into a pipette, preventing the dangerous practice of mouth pipetting.
## Q41
Question: Which one of the following is NOT a blood component?
Option A: Fresh frozen plasma
Option B: Hemolysate
Option C: Platelet concentrate
Option D: Plasma
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Fresh frozen plasma, platelet concentrates, and packed red cells are standard blood components prepared for transfusion. Hemolysate is simply the product of lysed (broken) red blood cells and is not a transfusable blood component.
## Q42
Question: What is the normal hemoglobin concentration in adult male?
Option A: 13-20 g/dl
Option B: 13-17 g/dl
Option C: 13-15 g/dl
Option D: 11-15 g/dl
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The normal reference range for hemoglobin in a healthy adult male is typically between 13.0 and 17.0 g/dL.
## Q43
Question: Which one of the following is NOT the site selected for collection of the blood?
Option A: Spleen
Option B: Arteries
Option C: Capillaries
Option D: Veins
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The spleen is an internal abdominal organ and is never a site for routine blood collection. Blood is routinely drawn from veins (venipuncture), capillaries (finger/heel stick), or arteries (ABG).
## Q44
Question: Which one of the following tests is NOT used to detect renal function?
Option A: Blood sugar
Option B: Blood urea
Option C: Serum creatinine
Option D: Blood urea nitrogen (BUN)
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Blood sugar (glucose) is a primary test for evaluating metabolic function and diagnosing diabetes. Blood urea, BUN, and serum creatinine are specific markers used to evaluate renal (kidney) function.
## Q45
Question: Hay's test detects ___________ in urine.
Option A: Bile salts
Option B: Blood
Option C: Ketone bodies
Option D: Bile pigment
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Hay's test is a surface tension test. When sulfur powder is sprinkled over urine containing bile salts, the surface tension is lowered, causing the sulfur powder to sink.
## Q46
Question: What is the gauge of the needle commonly used for venipuncture?
Option A: 26
Option B: 20
Option C: 21
Option D: 18
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: A 21-gauge needle (usually green-hubbed) is the most standard and widely used needle size for routine adult venipuncture, as it is large enough to prevent hemolysis but small enough to minimize patient discomfort.
## Q47
Question: Which of the following parasite is found in urine specimen?
Option A: Taenia sps
Option B: Trichomonas vaginalis
Option C: Trichuris trichiura
Option D: Round worm
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Trichomonas vaginalis is a flagellated protozoan that causes trichomoniasis (a sexually transmitted infection) and is frequently observed darting around in the microscopic examination of urine sediment.
## Q48
Question: What is the eosinophil count present in a normal DLC smear?
Option A: 1-10%
Option B: 6-10%
Option C: 1-6%
Option D: 5-10%
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: In a normal Differential Leukocyte Count (DLC) for a healthy adult, eosinophils typically constitute about 1% to 6% of the total circulating white blood cells.
## Q49
Question: Which of the following type of cholesterol is called as good cholesterol?
Option A: HDL
Option B: Chylomicrons
Option C: VLDL
Option D: LDL
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: High-Density Lipoprotein (HDL) is colloquially known as "good cholesterol" because it aids in transporting excess cholesterol from the peripheral tissues back to the liver for excretion.
## Q50
Question: Which of the following is a gram negative bacilli?
Option A: Staphylococcus aureus
Option B: Staphylococcus citreus
Option C: Streptococcus pneumoniae
Option D: Pseudomonas aeruginosa
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Pseudomonas aeruginosa is a classic gram-negative, rod-shaped bacterium (bacillus). The other three options are all gram-positive spherical bacteria (cocci).
## Q51
Question: What is the colour of normal CSF?
Option A: Colourless
Option B: Yellow
Option C: Light green
Option D: Brown
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Normal Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF) is perfectly clear and colourless, often described as having the appearance of distilled water.
## Q52
Question: Normal reticulocyte count in adults is __________.
Option A: 3-6%
Option B: 0.5-2.5%
Option C: 1-5%
Option D: 2-10%
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The reticulocyte count measures the percentage of immature red blood cells in circulation. The normal reference range for healthy adults is generally between 0.5% and 2.5%.
## Q53
Question: Cholesterol estimation is done by the following method:
Option A: Molisch method
Option B: Benedict's method
Option C: Zak's ferric chloride method
Option D: Jaffe's method
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Zak's method involves the reaction of cholesterol with ferric chloride and concentrated sulfuric acid to produce a colored complex, which is used for the colorimetric estimation of serum cholesterol.
## Q54
Question: Comma shaped bacteria are known as:
Option A: Staphylococci
Option B: Streptococci
Option C: Vibrio
Option D: Bacillus
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Bacteria belonging to the genus Vibrio (such as Vibrio cholerae) are characteristically curved or comma-shaped gram-negative rods.
## Q55
Question: A positive tuberculin test is indicated by an area of induration of:
Option A: < 5 mm in diameter
Option B: 6-9 mm in diameter
Option C: 10 mm in diameter
Option D: No induration
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: In a Mantoux (tuberculin) skin test, an induration (hard, raised area) of 10 mm or more is generally considered a positive result for individuals with normal immune systems or those in high-risk environments.
## Q56
Question: Diagnostic test used for Tuberculosis is:
Option A: Mantoux test
Option B: Dick Test
Option C: Schick test
Option D: Widal test
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The Mantoux test is the standard diagnostic skin test used to determine if a person has been infected with Mycobacterium tuberculosis.
## Q57
Question: Acid fast staining is used to detect:
Option A: Mycobacterium TB
Option B: Clostridium tetani
Option C: HIV/AIDS
Option D: Meningococci
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The Acid-Fast Bacillus (AFB) stain, such as the Ziehl-Neelsen stain, is specifically used to identify bacteria with high mycolic acid content in their cell walls, primarily Mycobacterium tuberculosis.
## Q58
Question: Method of sterilization used for culture media is:
Option A: Autoclave
Option B: Hot air oven
Option C: Pasteurization
Option D: None of the above
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Autoclaving (steam under pressure) is the most effective and widely used method for sterilizing liquid culture media, as it destroys all vegetative bacteria and highly resistant spores.
## Q59
Question: Shape of cocci:
Option A: Rod
Option B: Spherical
Option C: Comma
Option D: Spiral
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The morphological term "coccus" (plural: cocci) is used to describe bacteria that possess a spherical, round, or ovoid shape.

Option A: Syphilis

Option D: Tuberculosis

## Q61
Question: Name the anticoagulant present in purple coloured vacutainer tube:
Option A: Heparin
Option B: EDTA
Option C: Sodium citrate
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The purple (or lavender) top tube contains EDTA (Ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid), which chelates calcium to prevent clotting. It is the gold standard for hematology testing.
## Q62
Question: Ketone bodies in urine are:
Option A: Acetone
Option B: Beta hydroxy butyric acid
Option C: Both (A) and (B)
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The three main ketone bodies produced during fat metabolism are acetone, acetoacetic acid, and beta-hydroxybutyric acid, all of which can be excreted in the urine during ketosis.
## Q63
Question: Serum electrolytes are analysed using:
Option A: Colorimeter
Option B: Flame photometer
Option C: Spectrophotometer
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Flame photometry (and increasingly, Ion-Selective Electrodes or ISE) is the traditional analytical technique used to measure the concentrations of serum electrolytes like sodium, potassium, and lithium.
## Q64
Question: Temperature range used for autoclaving is:
Option A: 100°C for 10 min
Option B: 100°C for 30 min
Option C: 121°C for 15 min
Option D: 121°C for 30 min
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The standard parameters required to achieve complete sterilization in a laboratory autoclave are 121°C (250°F) at 15 pounds per square inch (psi) of pressure for 15 minutes.
## Q65
Question: The only cell in normal CSF is:
Option A: Neutrophil
Option B: Lymphocyte
Option C: Monocyte
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Normal CSF is virtually acellular. If any cells are present (usually 0-5 cells/µL), they are almost exclusively mature lymphocytes or occasional monocytes. The presence of neutrophils always indicates an abnormal process.
## Q66
Question: The solution used to fix a pap smear is:
Option A: Wright stain
Option B: Hematoxylin
Option C: Physiological saline
Option D: Cytospray
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: A commercial Cytospray (which primarily contains 95% ethyl alcohol) is used to immediately fix Pap smears, preventing air-drying artifacts and preserving the cellular architecture.
## Q67
Question: Presence of red blood cells in the urine is referred to as:
Option A: Hematuria
Option B: Hemoglobinuria
Option C: Anuria
Option D: Chyluria
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Hematuria is the medical term for the presence of intact red blood cells (erythrocytes) in the urine, which can indicate urinary tract bleeding.

Option B: 1.010–1.015
Option C: 1.010–1.030
Option D: 1.030–1.040

## Q69
Question: Name the test used to detect the presence of glucose in urine:
Option A: Fouchet's test
Option B: Benedict's test
Option C: Hay's test
Option D: Rothera's test
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Benedict's test relies on the reducing properties of sugars. When urine containing glucose is boiled with Benedict's reagent, it reduces cupric ions to cuprous oxide, forming a colored precipitate.
## Q70
Question: A compound microscope has how many lenses?
Option A: 1
Option B: 2
Option C: 3
Option D: 4
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: A standard laboratory compound microscope utilizes three primary lens systems to function effectively: the ocular lens (eyepiece), the objective lens, and the condenser lens system beneath the stage.
