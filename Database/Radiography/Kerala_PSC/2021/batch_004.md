## Q76
Question:
Rectum is approximately ___________ in length.
Option A:
8 to 11 cm
Option B:
10 to 13 cm
Option C:
12 to 15 cm
Option D:
15 to 18 cm
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
The rectum is the final segment of the large intestine, extending from the rectosigmoid junction to the anal canal. In an average adult, it measures approximately 12 to 15 centimeters in length. This anatomical measurement is important in clinical practice for procedures such as proctoscopy and various radiological contrast studies.

## Q80

Limiting mean dose to parotid glands to 45 Gy
Option B:
Amifostine therapy
Option C:
Both of the above
Option D:
None of the above
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Hard

## Q81
Question:
Intensity modulated radiotherapy includes _________ before plan acceptance.
Option A:
Inverse planning and defining constraints
Option B:
Forward planning and selecting beam parameters
Option C:
Optimization of treatment design
Option D:
Both 1 and 3
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
IMRT planning is fundamentally different from conventional forward planning. It utilizes inverse planning, where the clinician defines dose objectives and constraints first. The computer then runs optimization algorithms to determine the specific beam intensities required to meet those constraints. Therefore, both inverse planning and optimization are critical pre-acceptance steps.
## Q82
Question:
Which volume takes into consideration the organ motion and setup variations in IMRT planning?
Option A:
Gross tumor volume
Option B:
Clinical target volume
Option C:
Planning target volume
Option D:
Planning risk volume
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
In radiation oncology, the Gross Tumor Volume (GTV) is the visible tumor, and the Clinical Target Volume (CTV) includes areas of microscopic spread. The Planning Target Volume (PTV) is a mathematical margin added to the CTV to compensate specifically for daily patient setup errors and physiological organ motion during the course of treatment.
## Q83
Question:
The rationale for _________ fractionation is that reduction of overall treatment time decreases the opportunity for tumor cell regeneration.
Option A:
Accelerated fractionation radiotherapy
Option B:
Hyper fractionation radiotherapy
Option C:
Conventional fractionation radiotherapy
Option D:
Both 1 and 2
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
Accelerated fractionation involves delivering the total radiation dose over a significantly shorter overall time period than conventional treatment. The radiobiological goal is to prevent tumor cells from undergoing "repopulation" or regeneration, which can occur if the treatment course is prolonged, thereby improving local control rates for fast-growing tumors.
## Q84
Question:
Biological effect of concurrent chemo radiation include all except
Option A:
Shift of cell survival curves towards higher cell killing levels
Option B:
A decrease in tumor mass and re-oxygenation
Option C:
Increased apoptosis
Option D:
Favorer DNA repair
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Concurrent chemoradiation works because chemotherapy acts as a radiosensitizer, making tumor cells more susceptible to radiation damage. This synergy increases cell kill, promotes tumor shrinkage, and increases apoptosis. Critically, these drugs often work by inhibiting or interfering with the tumor's ability to repair radiation-induced DNA damage, rather than favoring repair.

55 Gy

65 Gy

B

## Q93
Question:
Left parotid gland radiation treatment is conventionally planned in which treatment position?
Option A:
Supine with neck extended
Option B:
Supine with neck flexed
Option C:
Supine with head turned to left
Option D:
Supine with head turned to right
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
To treat the left parotid gland effectively while sparing the contralateral side and midline structures, the patient is positioned supine. Turning the head to the right moves the left parotid gland away from the midline and facilitates optimal beam entry angles for parallel-opposed or wedge-pair field arrangements, increasing therapeutic accuracy.

## Q99
Question:
Early vocal cord cancers can be treated by all except
Option A:
Radical radiotherapy
Option B:
Trans oral laser micro surgery
Option C:
Trans oral robotic surgery
Option D:
Total laryngectomy
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
For early-stage vocal cord cancer (T1-T2 glottic carcinoma), the goal is to preserve voice quality. Options like radical radiotherapy, transoral laser microsurgery (TLM), and transoral robotic surgery (TORS) are all organ-preservation strategies. Total laryngectomy involves complete removal of the voice box and is reserved for advanced disease or salvage cases.

Question:

L3-L4 vertebral inter space
Option C:
L5-S1 vertebral inter space
Option D:
None of the above
