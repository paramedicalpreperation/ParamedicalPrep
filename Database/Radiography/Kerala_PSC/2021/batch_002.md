## Q26

Question:
Development of unexposed silver halide grains that do not contain the latent image is called

Option A:
oxidation

Option B:
photoelectric effect

Option C:
fog

Option D:
automatic exposure

Correct Answer:
C

Difficulty:
Medium

Explanation:
Photographic fog is defined as the completely unwanted, uniform background density that appears on a processed radiograph. It occurs entirely because unexposed silver halide crystals—which do not hold a latent image—are inadvertently reduced to black metallic silver due to excessive chemical development, heat, stray light, or age.

Option A:
one-half

Option B:
one-third

Option C:
one-fourth

Option D:
one-fifth

Correct Answer:
C

Difficulty:
Easy

Explanation:
Radiation intensity strictly adheres to the inverse square law, proving that dose is inversely proportional to the mathematical square of the distance from the point source. Therefore, moving twice the distance away automatically decreases the total radiation exposure to exactly one-fourth (1/2² = 1/4) of the original dose.
