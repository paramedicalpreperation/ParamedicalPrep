## Q76
Question:
All are parts of circle of willis except
Option A:
basilar artery
Option B:
ACA
Option C:
anterior cerebral artery
Option D:
vertebral artery
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
The Circle of Willis is a vascular ring at the base of the brain. It includes the Internal Carotid Arteries, Anterior Cerebral Arteries (ACA), Anterior Communicating Artery, Posterior Communicating Arteries, and Posterior Cerebral Arteries. The Basilar Artery feeds the circle, but the Vertebral Arteries join to form the basilar artery before reaching the circle itself.
## Q77
Question:
All are CT findings in hyperacute infarct except
Option A:
Normal
Option B:
Mass effect
Option C:
Hyperdense artery
Option D:
Obscuration of lentiform nucleus
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
In the hyperacute phase of a stroke (first few hours), CT scans are often normal. Early findings include the "hyperdense MCA sign" and the obscuration of the lentiform nucleus due to cytotoxic edema. Mass effect (sulcal effacement/midline shift) typically develops later as significant edema progresses.
## Q78
Question:
All are MR findings in immediate phase of cerebral infarction except
Option A:
absence of normal flow void
Option B:
perfusion alteration
Option C:
meningeal enhancement adjacent to infarct
Option D:
low apparent diffusion coefficient
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
Immediate MRI findings include restricted diffusion (low ADC values), loss of normal vascular flow voids, and altered perfusion. Meningeal enhancement is a later finding (usually 1–3 weeks) related to blood-brain barrier breakdown and neovascularization.

## Q84

Pyelonephritis
Option B:
Renal failure
Option C:
After renal trauma
Option D:
Vesicoureteric reflux
Correct Answer:
C

Medium
Explanation:

Option D:

## Q87
Question:
Which of the following is a local complication of Angiography?
Option A:
Air embolus
Option B:
False aneurysm
Option C:
Peripheral embolus
Option D:
Septicaemia
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
A false aneurysm (pseudoaneurysm) at the puncture site (usually the femoral artery) is a local complication occurring when the arterial wall fails to heal properly, resulting in a contained hematoma communicating with the artery. Air embolus and septicaemia are systemic complications.

## Q98
Question:
The CT number of liver tissue lie approximately how many HU?
Option A:
– 100 to – 200
Option B:
0 to 10
Option C:
40 to 90
Option D:
500 to 1000
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Normal liver tissue has a CT attenuation value (Hounsfield Unit) typically between 40 and 70 HU, making it slightly denser than other soft tissues like the spleen or blood. 40–90 HU is the commonly accepted range for healthy parenchyma.
## Q99
Question:
What is responsible for the contrast in MRI images?
Option A:
Proton density
Option B:
T1 relaxation
Option C:
T2 relaxation
Option D:
All of the above
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
MRI contrast is determined by a combination of the concentration of hydrogen nuclei (proton density) and their specific tissue-dependent magnetic recovery/decay properties, known as the T1 (longitudinal) and T2 (transverse) relaxation times.

Option A:
Window level

Window width
Option C:
Matrix

Voxel
