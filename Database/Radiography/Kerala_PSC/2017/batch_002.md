## Q26
Question:
All are factors which increase basic fog
Option A:
ageing of film
Option B:
processing temperature too low
Option C:
greater film speed
Option D:
too long a time of development
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
"Basic fog" (or chemical fog) is an increase in density not caused by X-ray exposure. Ageing, greater film speed (inherent), and excessive development time/temperature increase fog. Processing temperatures that are *too low* typically lead to under-development, which decreases density, making it the exception.
## Q27

Useful range of densities in a medical radiograph
Option A:
0.25 to 2
Option B:
2 to 3
Option C:
3 to 3.5
Option D:
4 to 6
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
The diagnostically useful range of optical densities on a medical radiograph—where the human eye can best distinguish shades of gray—is typically between 0.25 and 2.0. Densities above 2.5 are generally too dark for standard viewboxes.
## Q28
Question:
X-ray film boxes in a store should stand
Option A:
horizontal
Option B:
vertical
Option C:
left lateral
Option D:
right lateral
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
Radiographic films should always be stored vertically on their edge. Horizontal storage creates pressure on the film stack, which can lead to "pressure marks" or artifacts. Furthermore, horizontal storage makes retrieval difficult without bending the film.

## Q31

Option A:

Option B:
fixing
Option C:
rinse
Option D:
wash
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:

Explanation:

## Q32
Question:
Fixing solution must include all of the following except
Option A:
alkali
Option B:
acid
Option C:
stabilizer
Option D:
buffer
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Fixing solution is acidic. It requires an acid (like acetic acid) to stop the development process, a stabilizer (like sodium sulfite), and a buffer to maintain the pH. Alkali (like sodium carbonate) is a component of the *developer* solution, not the fixer.

## Q40

Option A:
carpal bones
Option B:
temporal bones
Option C:
sialography
Option D:
pelvic bones
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:

## Q41

High KVP exposure technique is used to demonstrate
Option A:

Option B:
mammography
Option C:
parasitic calcification
Option D:
calcification of arteries
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
High-kVp (e.g., 120+ kVp) techniques are used to reduce image contrast. Hypersthenic

Asthenic

Sthenic

Hyposthenic
Correct Answer:

Hard
