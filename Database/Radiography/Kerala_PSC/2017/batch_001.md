## Q22

Option A:
Mammography
Option B:
IVU
Option C:
Barium enema
Option D:
RGU
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Single-emulsion (single-sided) films are used in mammography to eliminate the parallax/crossover effect, which occurs in double-emulsion films and degrades spatial resolution. This allows for the high definition necessary to image minute microcalcifications.

## Q25
Question:
Over exposure corresponds to which part of characteristic curve of a typical medical x-ray film?
Option A:
Toe
Option B:
Straight ln.
Option C:
Shoulder
Option D:
Base
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
The H&D curve plots optical density versus log-exposure. The "shoulder" represents the region of high exposure where density reaches a maximum and levels off (saturation). Any exposure beyond this point results in minimal further increase in density (over-exposure).
