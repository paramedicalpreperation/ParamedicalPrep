## Q51
Question:
Chest x-ray P-A in full expiration taken in all of the following except
Option A:
pneumothorax
Option B:
pleural effusion
Option C:
inhaled foreign body
Option D:
diaphragmatic movement
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Expiration films are used to highlight pneumothorax (which becomes more apparent when lung volume decreases) and to detect air trapping caused by an inhaled foreign body. Diaphragmatic excursion is also assessed by comparing inspiration and expiration. Pleural effusion is typically imaged with decubitus or upright films, not expiration.
## Q52
Question:
Azygos vein is surrounded by how many layers of pleura?
Option A:
1
Option B:
2
Option C:
3
Option D:
4
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
The azygos vein arches over the root of the right lung to enter the superior vena cava. As it does so, it elevates the pleura, forming a fold called the azygos arch. Because the pleura reflects on both the superior and inferior surfaces of the vein, it creates a four-layered fold (two layers superior, two inferior) known as the "azygos mesentery."

## Q71
Question:
Colonic mucosa is better demonstrated by which investigation?
Option A:
BMFT
Option B:
Single contrast barium enema
Option C:
Double contrast barium enema
Option D:
Ultrasound
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
A double-contrast barium enema (using both barium and air) is superior for visualizing the colonic mucosa. It coats the wall in a thin layer, allowing for the clear identification of small polyps or early mucosal lesions that might be obscured in a single-contrast (barium-only) study.

midline shift can occur

can occur following trauma
Correct Answer:
