## Q76
Question:
In a postero-anterior view (PA view) of chest
Option A:
Cardiac shadow is magnified
Option B:
Scapula is projected over upper lobes
Option C:
Appears more diffusely opaque if overpenetrated
Option D:
Is adequate if 8-10 posterior ribs are seen above diaphragm
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
A PA chest X-ray is considered diagnostically adequate if the scapulae are rotated out of the lung fields, the spinous processes are aligned with the midline, and at least 8 to 10 posterior ribs are visible above the diaphragm, indicating full inspiration.
## Q77
Question:
The activator commonly used with rare earth phosphor gadolinium oxysulphide is
Option A:
Terbium
Option B:
Lanthanum
Option C:
Niobium
Option D:
Thulium
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
Gadolinium oxysulfide (Gd_2O_2S) is a common rare-earth phosphor used in intensifying screens. Terbium (Tb^{3+}) is the standard activator added to this crystal lattice, as it efficiently absorbs X-ray energy and converts it into high-intensity green light, drastically increasing system speed.

## Q79
Question:
SI unit of dose equivalent is
Option A:
Roentgens
Option B:
Gray
Option C:
Rad
Option D:
Sievert
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
The Sievert (Sv) is the SI unit of dose equivalent and effective dose, accounting for the biological effectiveness of different types of ionizing radiation on human tissue. The Gray (Gy) measures absorbed dose, while the Roentgen and Rad are traditional units.
## Q80
Question:
Which of the following is NOT a deterministic effect of radiation?
Option A:
Skin erythema
Option B:
Sterility
Option C:
Carcinogenesis
Option D:
Fetal abnormality
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Deterministic effects (like skin erythema, sterility, and fetal abnormalities) have a threshold dose, and their severity increases with dose. Carcinogenesis is a stochastic effect, meaning there is no safe threshold, and it is a probabilistic (chance-based) outcome.

## Q83
Question:
Personal monitoring devices include all EXCEPT
Option A:
Geiger Muller counter
Option B:
Film badges
Option C:
TLD badges
Option D:
Pocket dosimeters
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
Film badges, TLD badges, and pocket dosimeters are personal dosimeters worn by individuals to record cumulative dose. A Geiger-Muller counter is a radiation survey instrument, typically held by hand to detect radiation presence, not a device worn to record a worker's personal accumulated dose.

## Q85
Question:
Therapeutic range of electrons is given by depth of ____ depth dose.
Option A:
70%
Option B:
90%
Option C:
80%
Option D:
85%
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
The therapeutic range of an electron beam is clinically defined as the depth at which the dose drops to 90% of the maximum dose (D_{\text{max}}). This depth is the effective penetration depth used to ensure the tumor volume receives the intended prescription dose.
## Q86
Question:
Field shaping is done by all EXCEPT
Option A:
Custom blocking
Option B:
Wedge filters
Option C:
Independent jaws
Option D:
Multi leaf collimators
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Custom blocks, independent (asymmetric) jaws, and Multi-Leaf Collimators (MLCs) are all used to shape the radiation field to match the tumor volume. Wedge filters are used specifically to modify the *intensity* (dose distribution) across a beam, not to change the field shape itself.
## Q87
Question:
In linear accelerator, photon beam passes from the source till the exit from gantry head in the order
Option A:
Primary collimator → ion chamber → filter → MLC
Option B:
Primary collimator → filter → ion chamber → MLC
Option C:
Primary collimator → MLC → filter → ion chamber
Option D:
Filter → primary collimator → MLC → ion chamber
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
In a LINAC, the photon beam path from the target is: Primary collimator (shapes the beam), Flattening filter (uniforms intensity), Monitor Ion Chamber (measures dose), and finally the MLC (Multileaf Collimator) which defines the final treatment shape.

## Q89

Treatment verification

CT simulation
Option D:

## Q100
Question:
The phenomenon in which light continues to emit for sometime even after the radiation exposure has ended is called
Option A:
Fluorescence
Option B:
Quantum mottle
Option C:
Phosphorescence
Option D:
None
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Phosphorescence is the specific type of photoluminescence related to X-ray intensifying screens. Unlike fluorescence, which is instantaneous, a phosphorescent material stores energy and continues to glow (emit light) for a period after the excitation source (X-rays) has been removed.
