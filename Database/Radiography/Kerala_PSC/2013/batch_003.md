## Q51
Question:
_________ is the beam modifying device used for tilt of isodose curves from normal position.
Option A:
Wedge
Option B:
Diaphragm
Option C:
Collimator
Option D:
Cerrobend blocks
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
A wedge filter is a beam-modifying device made of high-density material that is thicker on one side than the other. Its primary function is to alter the intensity profile of the radiation beam, which results in the "tilting" or angling of the isodose curves to accommodate tissue surface contours.
## Q52
Question:
Lymphatics from uterus drain to all except
Option A:
External iliac lymph nodes
Option B:
Internal iliac lymph nodes
Option C:
Inguinal lymph nodes
Option D:
Mesenteric lymph nodes
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
The uterus has a rich lymphatic network. Drainage occurs primarily through the internal iliac, external iliac, and obturator nodes. The fundus also drains via the ovarian vessels to the para-aortic nodes. The inguinal nodes receive drainage from the round ligament. Mesenteric nodes are associated with the bowel, not the uterus.

## Q65
Question:
The speed of intensifying screen increases with all the following except
Option A:
Large crystal size
Option B:
Thick phosphor layer
Option C:
Reflective layer
Option D:
Absorptive layer
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
Intensifying screen speed is determined by how much light is produced per X-ray photon. Larger crystals and thicker phosphor layers increase efficiency. A reflective layer directs light toward the film. An "absorptive" (or light-absorbing) layer would decrease light output, thereby *reducing* screen speed, not increasing it.

Reduced development of exposed silver halide crystals
Option C:
Development of unexposed silver halide crystals
Option D:
Removal of unexposed silver halide crystals
Correct Answer:
C

Medium
Explanation:
Film fog is a generalized, undesirable increase in optical density across the film. It occurs when unexposed silver halide crystals are accidentally reduced to metallic silver during processing due to over-development, chemical contamination, light leaks, or exposure to inappropriate safelight wavelengths.

## Q71
Question:
Which of the following is an ionic contrast medium?
Option A:
Iopamidol
Option B:
Iohexol
Option C:
Iopromide
Option D:
Ioxaglate
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Ioxaglate is an ionic, low-osmolar contrast agent. While it is ionic, it is a dimer (two benzene rings), which gives it lower osmolality than older ionic monomers. Iopamidol, Iohexol, and Iopromide are non-ionic monomers, which are now preferred for their superior safety profile.
## Q72
Question:
Which is NOT true regarding mammography?

The X-ray tube has certain special design features, including a beryllium window and a molybdenum target
