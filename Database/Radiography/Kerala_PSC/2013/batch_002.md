## Q44
Question:
Geometric unsharpness can be reduced by
Option A:
Reducing focal spot size
Option B:
Increasing object-image distance
Option C:
Decreasing focus-image distance
Option D:
Using tube with large focal spot size
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
Geometric unsharpness (U_g) is defined by the formula U_g = (f \times OID) / SOD. Reducing the focal spot size (f) is the most direct way to reduce geometric unsharpness. Increasing the OID (Object-to-Image Distance) actually *increases* unsharpness, making option A the correct choice.

Compton scattering
Option D:

## Q50
Question:
In conventional radiation therapy, daily fraction dose given will be
Option A:
1 to 1.5 Gy
Option B:
1.8 to 2 Gy
Option C:
2 to 2.8 Gy
Option D:
None of the above
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
Conventional fractionation is the gold standard for external beam radiotherapy. It utilizes a dose of 1.8 to 2.0 Gray (Gy) per fraction, typically delivered five days a week. This schedule is designed to optimize the therapeutic ratio by allowing normal cells to repair radiation damage between fractions.
