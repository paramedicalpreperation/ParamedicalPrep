## Q76
Question:
Which of the following statements is not correct about barium enema?
Option A:
Air is insufflated when barium traverses splenic flexure
Option B:
Antispasmodic drug is indicated in any patient if marked /repeated spasm occurs during filling phase
Option C:
Additional view for sigmoid colon is prone film with tube angled cranially
Option D:
Delay of 7-10 days is advised after deep biopsy, snare polypectomy and acute colitis
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
For a barium enema, obtaining an unobstructed view of the elongated sigmoid colon requires unfolding its redundant loops. This is correctly achieved by taking an AP projection with a 30 to 40-degree cephalad (cranial) angulation, or a PA projection with a caudal angulation. A prone film with a cranial angle is incorrect and distorts the anatomy.

Option D:
Standardized uptake Value (SUV) is calculated at the end of study
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
In Positron Emission Tomography (PET), the administered radiotracer specifically undergoes beta-plus decay (emitting a positron), not standard beta-minus decay (which emits an electron). The emitted positron travels a short distance in tissue before colliding with an electron, causing an annihilation event that yields two 511 keV gamma photons emitted 180 degrees apart.
