## Q51
Question:
Which of the following statements is not correct about the Scaphoid fracture?
Option A:
PA wrist with ulnar deviation is used
Option B:
Place small pad under Metacarpophalangeal joint of hand to maintain wrist in close contact with film
Option C:
Centering point is radial styloid process
Option D:
Reexamination may be needed after 14 days of initial exposure
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
For a standard scaphoid view, the centering point is typically midway between the radial and ulnar styloid processes (the anatomical snuffbox), not just the radial styloid process. The other statements accurately describe standard radiographic techniques, including the use of ulnar deviation to elongate the bone and repeating the examination after 14 days for occult fractures.

## Q53
Question:
Which of the following statements is not correct about the dental radiography?
Option A:
Projection towards crown gives rise to elongation of the tooth shadow
Option B:
Some degree of foreshortening is permissible and elongation to be avoided
Option C:
Angulation of the tube for upper jaw premolars -tube occlusal plane angle 40 degrees
Option D:
Lower jaw incisors tube is centered upwards to symphysis menti at an angle of 25-30 degrees to occlusal plane
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
In dental radiography, incorrect vertical angulation leads to significant image distortion. Directing the central ray with excessive vertical angulation (projection towards the crown) causes foreshortening of the tooth image. Conversely, insufficient vertical angulation (projection towards the root or apex) is what actually causes elongation of the tooth shadow.

Option D:
Overnight fasting is advised before IV contrast study to prevent aspiration of stomach contents in the setting of contrast reaction
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
In cases of suspected tracheo-esophageal fistula, high-osmolar water-soluble iodinated contrast media is highly contraindicated.
