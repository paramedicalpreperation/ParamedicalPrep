## Q3
Question:
Which among the following is true about laws of electromagnetic induction?
Option A:
A change of magnetic flux linked with a conductor induces an electromagnetic force in the conductor
Option B:
The magnitude of the induced electromagnetic force is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux and to the area of the circuit
Option C:
The induced electromagnetic force is in such a direction that it always opposes the change of magnetic flux, which induced the electromagnetic force
Option D:
All of the above
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
The laws of electromagnetic induction, formulated by Faraday and Lenz, dictate that a changing magnetic flux induces an electromotive force (EMF) in a conductor. Faraday established that the magnitude is proportional to the rate of change, while Lenz’s law states the direction of the EMF always opposes the change that caused it. Therefore, all given statements are correct.

T2 weighted images
Option C:
Proton Density (PD) images

FLAIR images
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
In standard spin-echo MRI sequences, image contrast is manipulated by adjusting the Repetition Time (TR) and Echo Time (TE).
