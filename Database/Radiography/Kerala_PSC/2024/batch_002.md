## Q26
Question:
Median sagittal plane divides the body into:
Option A:
Right and left halves
Option B:
Anterior and posterior parts
Option C:
Superior and inferior parts
Option D:
Front and back parts
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
The median sagittal plane (also called the midsagittal plane) is an anatomical plane that passes vertically through the center of the body, dividing it into exactly equal right and left halves. This is a fundamental reference plane used for positioning patients for radiographic projections.
## Q27
Question:
Movement unsharpness can be reduced by:
Option A:
Shorter exposure time
Option B:
Small object to film distance
Option C:
Immobilization
Option D:
All of the above
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Movement unsharpness occurs when the patient or an anatomical part moves during an exposure. To minimize this, radiographers use shorter exposure times (to "freeze" motion), various immobilization devices (like sandbags or compression bands) to keep the patient still, and keep the OID small to maintain structural sharpness.

## Q31
Question:
What is the maximum whole body dose limit of radiation exposure to public per year?
Option A:
5 mSv
Option B:
10 mSv
Option C:
1 mSv
Option D:
20 mSv
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
To ensure the safety of the general public, the ICRP and AERB set the annual effective dose limit for members of the public at 1 mSv. This limit is set significantly lower than the occupational worker limit (20 mSv) to account for the fact that the public does not receive the training, monitoring, or clinical benefits of radiation work.
## Q32
Question:
Inverse square law is applied in:
Option A:
Doppler
Option B:
MRI
Option C:
Ultrasonography
Option D:
Radiography
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
The inverse square law is a fundamental principle in diagnostic radiography. It states that the intensity of the X-ray beam is inversely proportional to the square of the distance from the source. This law is critical for calculating adjustments to mAs when the Source-to-Image Distance (SID) is changed.

## Q35
Question:
As the pitch factor in CT increases, effective radiation dose to the body:
Option A:
Increases
Option B:
Decreases
Option C:
Remains same
Option D:
None of the above
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Pitch is the ratio of table travel per rotation to the X-ray beam width. Increasing the pitch means the table moves faster through the gantry. This reduces the time the X-ray beam spends on any single section of tissue, thereby reducing the total radiation dose delivered to the patient.
## Q36
Question:
Iodine particle ratio of low osmolar non ionic contrast media is:
Option A:
3:2
Option B:
6:1
Option C:
3:1
Option D:
2:1
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
Non-ionic monomeric contrast media (like Iohexol) are designed to provide high iodine content for radiopacity while keeping osmolality low. The iodine-to-particle ratio is 3:1 (three iodine atoms per one molecule), which significantly reduces the osmotic pressure exerted on tissues compared to older ionic contrast media.

20

30

40

60

## Q38
Question:
Which among the following is not a risk factor for iodinated contrast medium induced nephropathy?
Option A:
Dehydration
Option B:
High osmolality agents
Option C:
Diabetic nephropathy
Option D:
Hypertension
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Contrast-induced nephropathy (CIN) risk is highest in patients with existing renal impairment, dehydration, or diabetes. While hypertension is a health concern, it is not an independent, direct risk factor for CIN in the same way that renal hypoperfusion (dehydration) or pre-existing renal damage (diabetic nephropathy) are.
## Q39
Question:
Which among the following are deterministic radiation effects?
i. Cataract ii. Skin erythema iii. Oligozoospermia iv. Cancer
Option A:
i and ii
Option B:
i, ii and iii
Option C:
i, ii and iv
Option D:
i and iv
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
Deterministic effects have a threshold dose and the severity increases with dose. Cataracts, erythema, and oligospermia (temporary or permanent infertility) are well-known deterministic effects. Cancer (iv) is a stochastic effect, meaning it has no threshold and its occurrence is probabilistic, not deterministic.
## Q40
Question:
Which among the following is not used in CT for patient dose reduction?
Option A:
Automatic exposure control
Option B:
Dynamic collimators
Option C:
In-plane bismuth shielding
Option D:
Iterative reconstruction techniques
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
While bismuth shielding is used to protect radiosensitive organs (like the eyes or thyroid), it is not a tool for "patient dose reduction" in the clinical sense—it actually creates artifacts and forces the scanner to increase dose to compensate for the shield. AEC, collimation, and iterative reconstruction directly optimize the radiation delivered.

## Q47
Question:
The window used in mammography tube:
Option A:
Molybdenum
Option B:
Glass
Option C:
Aluminum
Option D:
Beryllium
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Mammography tubes use a Beryllium window. Unlike standard glass windows, which are relatively thick and absorb low-energy X-rays, Beryllium has a very low atomic number. This allows the essential low-energy X-ray spectrum required for high-contrast breast imaging to exit the tube without significant attenuation.
## Q48
Question:
Which of the following anatomic structure in children can mimic pathology in chest radiograph?
Option A:
Thyroid
Option B:
Thymus
Option C:
Heart
Option D:
Aorta
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
In infants and young children, the thymus is relatively large and often occupies the anterior superior mediastinum. On a chest X-ray, the thymic shadow can appear as a widened mediastinum or create a "sail sign," which can be easily misinterpreted by inexperienced observers as a tumor, mass, or mediastinal pathology.

Elbow

Shoulder

Foot
Option D:
Knee

## Q50
Question:
Maximum pressure used in mammography for breast compression is:
Option A:
300 N
Option B:
200 N
Option C:
250 N
Option D:
400 N
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Compression is essential for mammography quality, but it must be controlled for patient comfort and safety. Regulatory and clinical guidelines typically limit the maximum automatic compression force to 200 Newtons (approximately 45 pounds of force). Anything above this level is considered potentially harmful and unnecessary for adequate tissue immobilization.
