## Q1
Question:
Which of the following statements are true regarding x-rays?
i. Invisible
ii. Electrically charged
iii. No mass
iv. Can be optically focused
Option A:
i, ii and iii are correct
Option B:
i and iii are correct
Option C:
i, ii, iii and iv are correct
Option D:
ii and iv are correct
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
X-rays are high-energy electromagnetic radiation. They are electrically neutral (no charge), have no mass, and travel at the speed of light. Because they are not charged particles, they cannot be focused by traditional optical lenses or mirrors in the way visible light can.
## Q2
Question:
X ray beam used in diagnostic radiography are
Option A:
Monoenergetic
Option B:
Homogeneous
Option C:
Polyenergetic
Option D:
Unscattered
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
Diagnostic X-ray beams are polyenergetic or heterogeneous. Because the X-ray production process (bremsstrahlung) generates photons with a wide spectrum of energies, the resulting beam consists of various wavelengths and energies, rather than a single specific energy (monoenergetic).
## Q3
Question:
Wavelength and frequency are
Option A:
Inversely related
Option B:
Directly related
Option C:
Not related
Option D:
Partially related
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
For electromagnetic waves, frequency (f) and wavelength (\lambda) are inversely related according to the formula c = f\lambda, where c is the constant speed of light. As the frequency of a wave increases, its wavelength must decrease proportionally to maintain the speed of light.
## Q4
Question:
Focusing cup used in X ray tubes has
Option A:
No charge
Option B:
Positive charge
Option C:
Negative charge
Option D:
Dual charge
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
The focusing cup, which surrounds the cathode filament, is maintained at a negative potential relative to the filament. This negative charge electrostatically repels the electron cloud (space charge) inward, effectively narrowing the electron beam and focusing it onto a smaller area of the anode target.

## Q7
Question:
When electrons strike the anode target, how many percentage of kinetic energy is converted to X-rays approximately?
Option A:
1
Option B:
99
Option C:
50
Option D:
25
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
X-ray production is highly inefficient. Approximately 99% of the kinetic energy of the electrons colliding with the anode target is converted into heat due to excitation interactions with the target atoms. Only about 1% or less of the energy is successfully converted into X-ray photons.

Option B:
Glass envelope
Option C:
Aluminum
Option D:
Collimator mirror
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
Aluminum is the standard material used for added filtration in X-ray tubes. ## Q12
Question:
Vitamin D3 is converted to 25-OH vitamin D3 in which organ?
Option A:
Skin
Option B:
Spleen
Option C:
Kidney
Option D:
Liver
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Vitamin D (cholecalciferol) undergoes a two-step activation process. It is first hydroxylated in the liver to form 25-hydroxyvitamin D3 (calcidiol). Option A:
Foot
Option B:
Hand
Option C:
Shoulder
Option D:
Elbow
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
Böhler's angle is a radiological measurement used to assess for calcaneal (heel bone) fractures. It is measured on a lateral radiograph of the foot by drawing lines between specific points on the superior surface of the calcaneus. A reduced angle typically indicates a depressed fracture of the calcaneus.

## Q25
Question:
Plus density artifact on X ray film is seen as
Option A:
Yellow
Option B:
Red
Option C:
White
Option D:
Black
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
"Plus density" refers to any unwanted artifact on a processed radiograph that appears darker (more dense) than the surrounding diagnostic area. This is usually caused by localized light exposure, chemical contamination, or scratches that allow developer to react excessively with the silver halide. Black represents higher density.
