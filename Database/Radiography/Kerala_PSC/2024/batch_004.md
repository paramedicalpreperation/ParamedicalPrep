## Q76
Question:
Advantages of Iridium-192 as HDR source are all except:
Option A:
High specific activity
Option B:
Short half-life and quarterly source replacement
Option C:
Shielded effectively
Option D:
Small source size
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Iridium-192 is widely used in HDR brachytherapy due to its high specific activity and small physical source size, allowing for flexible catheter placement. However, its half-life is approximately 74 days; source replacement is typically required every 3 to 4 months (quarterly) due to decay, which is technically a logistical burden, not an advantage.
## Q77

Kv Cone beam CT
Option B:
MV Cone beam CT
Option C:
Real time tumour tracking system
Option D:
IMRT
Correct Answer:

## Q82

3D CRT
Option B:
Conventional radiation planning
Option C:
IMRT
Option D:
All of the above
Correct Answer:
C

Medium

## Q91
Question:
What is the primary characteristic of stochastic effects in Radiation Oncology?
Option A:
Directly proportional to radiation dose
Option B:
Increase linearly with radiation dose, but have a threshold
Option C:
Occur randomly and are independent of radiation dose
Option D:
Are deterministic and always occur above a certain dose threshold
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Stochastic effects are probabilistic in nature. There is no known "safe" threshold dose below which the effect is impossible. The likelihood (probability) of the effect (like cancer) occurring increases with the dose, but the severity of the cancer itself is independent of the dose that initially caused it.

## Q93
Question:
What is the effect of doubling the distance from a radiation source on radiation exposure?
Option A:
Exposure increases by a factor of 4
Option B:
Exposure decreases by a factor of 2
Option C:
Exposure decreases by a factor of 4
Option D:
Exposure remains the same
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
According to the Inverse Square Law, radiation intensity is inversely proportional to the square of the distance from the source. If the distance is doubled (×2), the intensity is reduced by the factor of 2 squared (2² = 4). Thus, the exposure decreases by a factor of four.

## Q98
Question:
A patient with cervical cancer is undergoing External beam radiation therapy. She had a treatment break of 10 days due to side effects. What is the most significant concern regarding the treatment gap?
Option A:
Reduced tumor control due to accelerated re population
Option B:
Increased risk of late toxicity
Option C:
Decreased efficacy of concurrent chemotherapy
Option D:
Loss of radio-sensitization effect from concurrent chemotherapy
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
Radiation therapy requires a continuous schedule to be effective. A 10-day treatment gap allows aggressive tumor cells to undergo "accelerated repopulation," where they divide rapidly to compensate for the radiation damage. This significantly increases the risk of local treatment failure, making tumor control much harder to achieve.
