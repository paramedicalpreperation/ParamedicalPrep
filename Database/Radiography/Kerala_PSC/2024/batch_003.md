## Q52
Question:
The energy of the characteristic X-ray is:
Option A:
equal to the maximum energy of the incident electron
Option B:
always less than the maximum energy of the incident electron
Option C:
equal to the difference in the binding energies of two orbital electrons involved
Option D:
equal to the sum of the binding energies of two orbital electrons involved
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Characteristic X-rays are emitted when an outer-shell electron fills a vacancy in an inner shell. The energy of the emitted photon is exactly equal to the difference in the binding energies of the two specific electron shells involved in the transition.

## Q53
Question:
The half life of Iridium-192 is 74 days. The fraction of atoms that will remain after 148 days:
Option A:
75%
Option B:
50%
Option C:
25%
Option D:
12.5%
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Since the half-life is 74 days, 148 days represents exactly two half-lives (148/74 = 2). After one half-life, 50% of the atoms remain. After two half-lives, 50% of the remaining 50% remains, which is 25% of the original amount.

## Q57
Question:
What is the purpose of the flattening filter in linear accelerator?
Option A:
To collimate the X ray beam
Option B:
To make the X ray beam intensity uniform across the field
Option C:
To monitor field symmetry and dose rate
Option D:
To flatten the electron field
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
In photon mode, the X-ray beam produced by a LINAC is naturally "forward-peaked" (higher intensity at the center). ## Q59

Which of the following is false regarding bolus?

Correct Answer:
C

Hard
Explanation:
Bolus is a tissue-equivalent material used to fill surface irregularities or increase surface dose. Megavoltage photon beams exhibit "skin-sparing" (the dose is lower at the skin and peaks deeper). Optic nerve
Option C:
Mandibular joint
Option D:
Skin
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Organs are classified as "parallel" or "serial" based on how they respond to radiation. A serial organ, such as the optic nerve or spinal cord, functions like a chain; if one small part is damaged, the entire organ function is compromised. Parallel organs, like the parotid gland or lungs, have functional subunits that can tolerate local damage.

## Q73
Question:
Inverse treatment planning is used in:
Option A:
3DCRT
Option B:
Conventional radiation planning
Option C:
IMRT
Option D:
All of the above
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
In conventional and 3DCRT planning (forward planning), the clinician manually places beams and modifies their parameters to achieve a dose distribution. In IMRT, "inverse" planning is used: the desired dose distribution is defined first, and the computer system mathematically calculates the optimal beam intensities to achieve it.
