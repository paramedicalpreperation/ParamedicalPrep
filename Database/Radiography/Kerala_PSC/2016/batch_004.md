## Q76

Among the following which is used in the treatment of prostatic malignancy?

Cobalt – 60
Option B:
Iodine – 130
Option C:
Cesium – 131
Option D:
Technetium – 99
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:

## Q80
Question:
Which is the type of interaction between X-rays and matter that do not produce ionisation?
Option A:
Pair production
Option B:
Photoelectric effect
Option C:
Coherent scattering
Option D:
Compton scattering
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Coherent (or classical/Thomson) scattering involves an incident photon interacting with an entire atom. The photon's direction is changed, but it does not lose energy and does not cause ionization (no electrons are ejected). It is a minor interaction at standard diagnostic energy levels.
## Q81
Question:
Among the following, which is NOT the end product of photoelectric effect?
Option A:
Photo electron
Option B:
Characteristic radiation
Option C:
Positive ion
Option D:
Proton
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Photoelectric effect involves an incident photon ejecting an inner-shell electron (the photo-electron), leaving a vacancy in the atom (the positive ion). This vacancy is then filled by an outer-shell electron, resulting in characteristic radiation. Protons are nuclear particles and are not involved in this electronic interaction.
## Q82
Question:
One gray is ____ rads.
Option A:
1000
Option B:
10000
Option C:
10
Option D:
100
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
The Gray (Gy) is the SI unit for absorbed dose, while the rad is the traditional unit. By definition, 1 Gray is equal to 1 Joule per kilogram, and 1 rad is 0.01 Joule per kilogram. Therefore, 1 Gray is equivalent to exactly 100 rads.
## Q83

10 - 15 GY
Option C:
5 - 8 GY
Option D:
15 - 20 GY
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:

Contrast enhanced MRI
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:

## Q92
Question:
The energy of the characteristic X-ray produced depends on
Option A:
Effective energy of the X-ray beam
Option B:
Kvp
Option C:
mas
Option D:
Atomic number of the target
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Characteristic radiation is produced by electron transitions between specific atomic shells of the target material. The energy of these photons is fixed and determined entirely by the binding energy differences of the specific target atom (e.g., Tungsten). Thus, it is determined by the target's atomic number, not by the tube settings.

## Q95
Question:
The average life of a radioactive materials is
Option A:
0.693/T
Option B:
0.693/T 1/2
Option C:
1.44 T 1/2
Option D:
1.44/T 1/2
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
The average life (T_a) is the reciprocal of the decay constant (\lambda). Since \lambda = 0.693 / T_{1/2}, the average life is the reciprocal, which is T_{1/2} / 0.693. Since 1/0.693 is approximately 1.44, the formula is 1.44 \times T_{1/2}.
## Q96
Question:
Amorphous selenium is a
Option A:
Photo conductor
Option B:
Photocathode
Option C:
Semiconductor
Option D:
Thermoluminescent material
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
Amorphous selenium is used in direct-conversion flat panel detectors. It acts as a photoconductor; when it is struck by X-rays, it directly creates electron-hole pairs. ## Q98
Question:
One of the interactions of ultrasound with matter - reflection depends on
Option A:
Frequency of sound
Option B:
Viscosity of the conducting medium
Option C:
Relaxation time of the medium
Option D:
Acoustic impedance of the tissue
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Ultrasound reflection occurs at the interface between two media. The amount of reflection depends specifically on the difference in "acoustic impedance" (Z = \rho \times c, where \rho is density and c is speed of sound) between those two tissues. Large differences lead to strong echoes, while small differences result in minimal reflection.

Hydroquinone

Sodium hydroxide
Option D:
Sodium carbonate
Correct Answer:
