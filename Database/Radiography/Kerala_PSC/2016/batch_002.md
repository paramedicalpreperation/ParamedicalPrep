## Q26
Question:
Which chemical is commonly used as fixing agent?
Option A:
Hydroquinone
Option B:
Sodium thio sulphate
Option C:
Sodium sulphite
Option D:
Potassium bromide
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
Sodium thiosulfate is the primary fixing agent used in radiographic film processing. Its chemical function is to dissolve and clear away the undeveloped, unexposed silver halide crystals from the film emulsion, thereby making the latent image permanent and resistant to further light exposure.

## Q33
Question:
In radiography subject contrast is increased by all EXCEPT
Option A:
Low Kvp
Option B:
Contrast media
Option C:
Scattered radiation
Option D:
Collimation
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Subject contrast is the difference in radiation intensity reaching the image receptor due to differential absorption by patient tissues. Low kVp, contrast media, and collimation all increase contrast. Scattered radiation, however, adds uniform fog to the image, which decreases the contrast between tissues.
