## Q76
Question:
Iohexol is:
Option A:
Nonionic dimer
Option B:
Nonionic monomer
Option C:
Ionic dimer
Option D:
Ionic monomer
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
Iohexol is a low-osmolar, non-ionic, water-soluble contrast medium. Chemically, it is classified as a monomeric molecule. Non-ionic monomers like Iohexol were developed to reduce the risk of adverse reactions compared to older high-osmolar ionic monomers by significantly lowering the osmolality of the contrast solution.

Correct Answer:

## Q81
Question:
Deterministic effects of radiation include all except:
Option A:
Cataract
Option B:
Hair loss
Option C:
Leukemia
Option D:
Skin discolouration
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Deterministic (tissue) effects occur only after a threshold dose is exceeded; their severity is dose-dependent. Cataracts, epilation (hair loss), and erythema (skin discoloration) are deterministic. Leukemia is a stochastic effect; it is a probabilistic event where the chance of occurrence increases with dose, but the severity does not.

## Q91
Question:
Which of the following statement is true regarding photostimulable phosphor plates?
Option A:
Made up of Europeum activated barium fluorohalide
Option B:
Final image density depends only on the exposure factor used
Option C:
Image is read by continuous sampling
Option D:
Double emulsion film is used as image plate
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
CR imaging plates use a storage phosphor, typically Europium-doped barium fluorohalide (BaFX:Eu²⁺). When exposed to X-rays, electrons are trapped in the phosphor lattice. During the reading phase, a scanning laser stimulates these electrons to release the trapped energy as visible light, which is then captured and digitized to form the image.

## Q95
Question:
As the pitch factor in CT decreases, effective radiation dose to the body:
Option A:
Increases
Option B:
Decreases
Option C:
Remains same
Option D:
None of the above
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
Pitch is the ratio of table travel distance per rotation to the X-ray beam width. A low pitch (e.g., <1) means there is overlapping exposure as the table moves slowly, which increases the total radiation dose delivered to the patient. A higher pitch reduces the overlap and thus lowers the dose.
## Q96
Question:
Which among the following statement is true regarding the CT number?
Option A:
The CT number of white matter and gray matter of brain is same
Option B:
CT number of air is 0
Option C:
It represents the linear attenuation coefficient in each pixel
Option D:
The CT number of water is 1000
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
The CT number (Hounsfield Unit) is a quantitative scale derived directly from the linear attenuation coefficient of the tissue within a voxel. Water is set to 0 HU, air to -1000 HU, and denser structures like bone have higher positive values. White and gray matter have different densities and therefore different CT numbers.

## Q99
Question:
In MRI, scan time depends on:
Option A:
i and iv
Option B:
i, iii and iv
Option C:
i, ii and iii
Option D:
All of the above
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:

## Q100
Question:
Which among the following is not an artifact in fluoroscopy?
Option A:
Lag
Option B:
Vignetting
Option C:
Pincushion distortion
Option D:
Beam hardening
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
Lag, vignetting, and pincushion distortion are well-documented artifacts specific to the image intensifier chain in fluoroscopy. Beam hardening is a phenomenon primarily associated with CT imaging, where lower-energy photons are preferentially absorbed as the beam passes through an object, causing streaking artifacts; it is not a primary fluoroscopy artifact.
