## Q51
Question:
Function of fixer in film processing is:
Option A:
Dissolve unexposed and underdeveloped silver halide crystals
Option B:
Preserve the film image
Option C:
Hardens the film emulsion
Option D:
All of the above
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
The fixing solution serves multiple critical functions: it dissolves the remaining unexposed silver halide crystals (clearing), hardens the gelatin emulsion to prevent mechanical damage, and neutralizes the developer to stop the processing reaction, effectively preserving the image for permanent storage.
## Q52
Question:
X-ray film is most sensitive to which part of the light spectrum?
Option A:
Blue - Green
Option B:
Yellow - Red
Option C:
Purple - Green
Option D:
Orange - Red
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Most modern radiographic films are "orthochromatic," meaning they are specially sensitized to be highly sensitive to the blue-green portion of the light spectrum. This corresponds directly to the light emitted by modern rare-earth intensifying screens (like gadolinium oxysulfide), ensuring maximum system speed and efficiency.

## Q57

Built in safety + operational safety

Built in safety + laser safety
Option D:
All of the above
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:

1.5 mm
Option B:
2.5 mm
Option C:
0.5 mm
Option D:
4.0 mm
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
To prevent "fogging" of unexposed X-ray film stored or being processed in the darkroom, the walls must provide adequate radiation shielding. ## Q61
Question:
The following position is used for storage of unexposed X-ray films to reduce artifacts:
Option A:
Horizontal
Option B:
Vertical
Option C:
Both 1 and 2
Option D:
None of the above
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Unexposed radiographic films should always be stored vertically on their edge. Storing them horizontally (flat) can cause pressure marks from the weight of other boxes, while also making it more difficult to retrieve the film without bending it, which can introduce artifacts like "crescent marks."

## Q68
Question:
Geometric unsharpness can be reduced by:
Option A:
Reducing the object film distance
Option B:
Using small focal spot size
Option C:
Using large focal spot size
Option D:
Both 1 and 2
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Geometric unsharpness (penumbra) is determined by the focal spot size, the Source-to-Object Distance (SOD), and the Object-to-Image Distance (OID). To achieve maximum sharpness, one must use the smallest practical focal spot size and keep the object as close to the image receptor as possible (reducing OID/OFD).

Mandible radiography

Cervical spine lateral view
Option D:
Both 1 and 3

## Q75
Question:
Atomic number of Barium is:
Option A:
63
Option B:
53
Option C:
56
Option D:
75
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
Barium (Z=56) is used as a radiopaque contrast agent because its relatively high atomic number results in a high probability of photoelectric absorption of X-rays. This creates the high-contrast "white" appearance on radiographs that clearly outlines the gastrointestinal tract lumen against surrounding soft tissues.
