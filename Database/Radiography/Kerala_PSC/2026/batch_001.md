## Q1
Question:
For heavier nuclei, stability requires:
Option A:
Fewer neutrons than protons
Option B:
Equal neutrons and protons
Option C:
More neutrons than protons
Option D:
No neutrons
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
As the atomic number increases, the electrostatic repulsion between the positively charged protons grows significantly. To maintain nuclear stability, heavier nuclei require an increasing ratio of neutrons to protons, as neutrons provide the strong nuclear force to counteract the repulsion without adding any further electrostatic charge.
## Q2
Question:
The half-life of cobalt-60 is approximately 5.26 years. Its mean life is approximately:
Option A:
3.5 years
Option B:
5.26 years
Option C:
7.6 years
Option D:
10 years
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
The mean life (\tau) of a radioactive isotope is related to its half-life (T_{1/2}) by the formula \tau = \frac{T_{1/2}}{0.693} (or \approx 1.44 \times T_{1/2}). Therefore, 1.44 \times 5.26 \approx 7.57, which is approximately 7.6 years.

## Q4
Question:
For a tungsten target (Z = 74), what voltage is required to get efficiency 0.01 (1%)?
Option A:
100 kV
Option B:
150 kV
Option C:
200 kV
Option D:
250 kV
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
The efficiency of X-ray production (\eta) is approximately \eta \approx 9 \times 10^{-10} \times Z \times V (where V is the tube voltage in volts). For Z=74 and \eta=0.01: 0.01 = 9 \times 10^{-10} \times 74 \times V. Solving for V gives approximately 150,000 volts, or 150 \text{ kV}.
## Q5

Option A:
Lower energy
Option B:
Higher average energy
Option C:
No energy change
Option D:
Reduced penetration
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
Added filtration (usually aluminum) absorbs the low-energy, long-wavelength photons that have insufficient energy to reach the image receptor. By removing these "soft" photons, the average energy of the remaining X-ray beam is shifted higher, a process known as "beam hardening."

Z³ dependence

## Q22
Question:
When the distance from the X ray source is doubled, the beam intensity is
Option A:
Reduced by half
Option B:
Increased by half
Option C:
Reduced by one fourth
Option D:
Increased by one fourth
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
According to the Inverse Square Law (I \propto 1/d^2), if the distance is doubled (2d), the intensity decreases by a factor of 2 squared (2^2 = 4). Therefore, the intensity is reduced to one-fourth of its original value.
