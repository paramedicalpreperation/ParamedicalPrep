## Q26

Knee
Option B:
Skull
Option C:
Chest
Option D:
Hip
Correct Answer:
D

## Q29

Electrically neutral
Option C:
Highly penetrating invisible rays
Option D:
Can be focussed by lens
Correct Answer:

## Q33
Question:
Effective shielding material for beta radiation is
Option A:
Lead
Option B:
Paper
Option C:
Perspex
Option D:
Copper
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Beta particles are electrons. When they strike high-atomic-number materials like lead, they produce secondary X-rays (bremsstrahlung). Therefore, low-atomic-number materials like Perspex (acrylic/lucite) are preferred for shielding beta sources, as they effectively slow the electrons with minimal production of bremsstrahlung.

## Q50
Question:
Which of the following statements correctly matches the type of radiation dose with its SI unit?
Option A:
Absorbed dose is measured in Gray (Gy), while equivalent and effective doses are measured in Sievert (Sv)
Option B:
Absorbed dose is measured in Sievert (Sv), while effective dose is measured in Gray (Gy)
Option C:
Equivalent dose is measured in Gray (Gy), while absorbed dose is measured in Coulomb/kg
Option D:
Effective dose is measured in Gray (Gy), while absorbed dose is measured in Sievert (Sv)
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Gray (Gy) is the SI unit for *absorbed* dose (energy per unit mass). Sievert (Sv) is the SI unit for *equivalent* and *effective* dose, which are weighted to represent biological harm. Coulomb/kg (C/kg) is the SI unit for *exposure* (charge per unit mass in air).
