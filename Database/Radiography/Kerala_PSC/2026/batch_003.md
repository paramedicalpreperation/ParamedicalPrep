## Q51
Question:
Importance factors that reduce patient exposure during radiography includes all EXCEPT
Option A:
Low Kvp
Option B:
Beam filtration
Option C:
Beam collimation
Option D:
High speed film screen system
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Using "low kVp" techniques significantly increases the patient's entrance skin dose, as lower-energy X-rays are more easily absorbed by the patient's tissues. Filtration, collimation, and high-speed film-screen systems are all standard techniques specifically used to *reduce* patient dose.
## Q52
Question:
X-ray receptors in a CR (Computer Radiography) system contains
Option A:
Silver bromide
Option B:
Calcium tungstate
Option C:
Zinc cadmium sulphide
Option D:
Europium doped barium fluro halide
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Computed Radiography (CR) uses a storage phosphor imaging plate. The active component in this plate is typically Europium-doped barium fluorohalide (BaFX:Eu²⁺). When exposed to X-rays, electrons are trapped in the phosphor lattice, forming a latent image that is subsequently read out by a scanning laser.

## Q56
Question:
The processional frequency of hydrogen for field strength 1.5 Tesla is
Option A:
21.3 MHz
Option B:
42.6 MHz
Option C:
6.39 MHz
Option D:
63.9 MHz
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
The Larmor frequency is calculated using the gyromagnetic ratio (\gamma) and the magnetic field strength (B_0). For hydrogen (^{1}\text{H}), \gamma is approximately 42.6 MHz/Tesla. Thus, for a 1.5T magnet, the frequency is 42.6 \times 1.5 = 63.9 MHz.
## Q57
Question:
In T_1 weighted images parameters are
Option A:
Short TR and short TE
Option B:
Short TR and long TE
Option C:
Long TE and long TR
Option D:
Long TR and short TE
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
T1 weighting is achieved by using a "short" Repetition Time (TR) to allow for T1 recovery differences, and a "short" Echo Time (TE) to minimize the influence of T2 relaxation effects, ensuring that the image contrast is primarily based on the T1 properties of the tissues.

## Q59
Question:
The CT artefacts produced due to heterogeneous X-ray beam are called
Option A:
Motion artefacts
Option B:
Beam hardening artefacts
Option C:
Streak artefacts
Option D:
Ring artefacts
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
"Beam hardening" occurs because an X-ray beam is polyenergetic. As the beam passes through an object, lower-energy photons are absorbed more quickly, "hardening" the beam. If the reconstruction algorithm assumes a monoenergetic beam, this heterogeneity causes dark streaks or bands in the image, known as beam hardening artifacts.
## Q60
Question:
The input fluorescent screen in newer image intensifier is made up of
Option A:
Calcium tungstate
Option B:
Cesium iodide
Option C:
Cadmium sulphate
Option D:
Zinc sulphate
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
In modern image intensifiers, the input screen is coated with a layer of needle-shaped Cesium Iodide (CsI) crystals. These crystals act as efficient scintillators that convert incoming X-ray photons into light photons, and their needle-like structure minimizes light spread, significantly improving the image resolution compared to older powder-based screens.
## Q61
Question:
In an image intensifier if the flux gain is 50 and minification is 80, what is brightness gain ?
Option A:
4000
Option B:
130
Option C:
30
Option D:
1.6
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
Brightness gain is calculated as the product of flux gain and minification gain. 50 \times 80 = 4000. Flux gain refers to the increase in the number of light photons, while minification gain relates to the compression of the electron image from a large input phosphor to a smaller output phosphor.
## Q62
Question:
If height of lead stripes is 2 mm and the distance between lead stripes is 0.25 mm, what is the grid ratio ?
Option A:
0.5
Option B:
8
Option C:
10
Option D:
4
Correct Answer:
B
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Grid ratio is defined as the height of the lead strips (h) divided by the distance between them (d). Ratio = h / d. Therefore, 2 \text{ mm} / 0.25 \text{ mm} = 8. A grid ratio of 8:1 is a standard value used in diagnostic radiography.
