## Q26
Question: Blunt and penetrating trauma of the abdomen can result in:
Option A: internal haemorrhage
Option B: appendicitis
Option C: pancreatitis
Option D: ectopic pregnancy
Correct Answer: A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Blunt or penetrating abdominal trauma often leads to the rupture of solid organs or laceration of major vessels, leading to immediate internal hemorrhage.
## Q27
Question: What is the primary advantage of spiral (helical) CT over traditional sequential CT?
Option A: Faster scan times and continuous data acquisition
Option B: Reduced patient dose
Option C: Better contrast resolution
Option D: Improved spatial resolution
Correct Answer: A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Helical CT allows the gantry to rotate continuously while the table moves, enabling rapid, continuous data acquisition and reducing motion artifacts compared to sequential CT.

## Q29
Question: What type of generator produces an x-ray exposure in the shortest possible exposure time by operating the x-ray tube at its maximum kilowatt rating during the entire exposure?
Option A: Conventional 2 Pulse X-ray generator
Option B: Falling load generator
Option C: Battery-powered generator
Option D: Capacitor discharge generator
Correct Answer: B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: A falling load generator uses the maximum power capacity of the X-ray tube at the start of the exposure and then decreases the current (mA) as the exposure progresses to maintain safe thermal limits.

## Q36
Question: What does the term 'CT number' or 'Hounsfield Unit (HU)' represent in computed tomography?
Option A: The speed of the X-ray tube rotation
Option B: The reconstruction algorithm used
Option C: The relative density of tissues compared to water
Option D: The amount of radiation dose received by the patient
Correct Answer: C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Hounsfield units (HU) quantify radiodensity, with water defined as 0 HU, air as -1000 HU, and dense bone as +1000 HU.
