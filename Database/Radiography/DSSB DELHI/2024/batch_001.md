## Q1
Question:
Which of the following technical advancements in multi-detector CT (MDCT) allows for isotropic imaging, enabling high-quality three-dimensional reconstructions without compromising image resolution?
Option A:
Increased gantry rotation speed
Option B:
Adaptive Statistical Iterative Reconstruction (ASIR)
Option C:
Dynamic z-flying focal spot technology
Option D:
Iterative Reconstruction Algorithms
Correct Answer:
C
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
Dynamic z-flying focal spot technology allows for the focal spot to rapidly switch positions in the z-axis (longitudinal) direction, which effectively doubles the number of projections. This provides higher spatial resolution and enables true isotropic voxel data for superior 3D reconstructions.
## Q2
Question:
The radiation emission accompanying electron deceleration is called:
Option A:
Bremsstrahlung Emission
Option B:
Positron Annihilation
Option C:
Pair Production
Option D:
Compton Scattering
Correct Answer:
A
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
Bremsstrahlung (or "braking radiation") is produced when high-speed electrons are decelerated by the intense electric field of the target nucleus. As the electron loses kinetic energy, that energy is emitted as an X-ray photon.

## Q7
Question:
Which of the following in cardiac CT has significantly reduced patient radiation exposure while maintaining image quality?
Option A:
Retrospective ECG Gating
Option B:
Dual-Energy CT (DECT)
Option C:
Dual-Source CT (DSCT)
Option D:
Prospective ECG Gating
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
Prospective ECG gating (step-and-shoot) triggers the X-ray exposure only during a specific portion of the cardiac cycle (usually diastole) and leaves the tube off during the rest of the cycle, which significantly lowers radiation dose compared to retrospective gating where the tube remains on throughout the entire cardiac cycle.

## Q19
Question:
The electric field of the incident photon's electromagnetic wave expends energy, causing all of the electrons in the scattering atom to oscillate in phase called:
Option A:
Pair production
Option B:
Compton
Option C:
Photo-electric effect
Option D:
Rayleigh
Correct Answer:
D
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
Rayleigh scattering (coherent scattering) occurs when the incident photon's electric field causes all electrons in the atom to oscillate in phase. The atom then emits a photon of the same energy in a different direction; no energy is absorbed by the atom.
