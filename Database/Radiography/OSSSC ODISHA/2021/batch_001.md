## Q1
Question:
Why is contrast used in CT scan?
Option A:
To suppress particular tissues
Option B:
To enhance a particular tissue
Option C:
To ensure correct tissue is being imaged
Option D:
To reduce bone interference
Correct Answer:
Option B
Difficulty:
Easy
Explanation:
Contrast agents are used in CT scans to increase the attenuation of specific tissues, thereby enhancing their visibility and contrast against surrounding structures.

## Q12
Question:
How much of the generated energy is actually utilized for taking an X-ray?
Option A:
0.1%
Option B:
1%
Option C:
10%
Option D:
100%
Correct Answer:
Option A
Difficulty:
Hard
Explanation:
In X-ray production, more than 99% of the kinetic energy of electrons is converted to heat, with less than 1% becoming X-ray energy (approximately 0.1% for diagnostic X-rays).

Option B:
Contrast scattering
Option C:
Compton scattering
Option D:
Pair production
Correct Answer:
Option A
Difficulty:
Medium
Explanation:
