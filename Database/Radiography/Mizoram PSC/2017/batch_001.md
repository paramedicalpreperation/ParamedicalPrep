## Q1
Question: Which of the following is a wrong statement for Computed Radiography systems:
Option A: Europium is the activator element
Option B: Image reading is performed using laser
Option C: Photo stimulable phosphor is silver halide
Option D: Cassettes are used.
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: In computed radiography (CR), the photostimulable phosphor is typically made of barium fluorohalide, not silver halide, which is used in conventional film-screen radiography.

## Q2
Question: Enteroclysis is an investigation of
Option A: Esophagus
Option B: Small Bowel
Option C: Large bowel
Option D: Hepato-biliary tree
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Enteroclysis is a fluoroscopic procedure that specifically examines the small bowel by introducing contrast directly into the duodenum or jejunum via a tube.

## Q3
Question: TFT array in Flat panel detector DR systems is made of :
Option A: Coarse Antimony
Option B: Amorphous Silicon
Option C: Powdered Bismuth
Option D: Dry Manganese
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: In indirect flat-panel digital radiography systems, the Thin-Film Transistor (TFT) array is typically constructed using amorphous silicon (a-Si) to convert light from a scintillator into an electrical signal.

## Q4
Question: CCD means:
Option A: Charged Couple Detector
Option B: Continuous Charged Detector
Option C: Conventional charged Device
Option D: Continuous Conventional Device
Correct Answer: Option A

Explanation: CCD stands for Charge-Coupled Device (or Detector), which is a light-sensitive integrated circuit used in various digital imaging systems, including indirect digital radiography.

## Q5

## Q6
Question: Grids used in Mammography is made up of
Option A: Lead
Option B: Iron
Option C: Carbon
Option D: Platinum
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Mammography grids often use carbon fiber or wood for the interspace material (and sometimes covers) because they are highly radiolucent, which is essential to minimize attenuation of the low-energy X-rays used in mammography. The absorbing strips are still lead, but the overall structure heavily features carbon.

---

## Q7
Question: Digital Radiography (DR) systems are of the following types
Option A: Slot Scanning type
Option B: Flat panel detector type
Option C: Photon counting type
Option D: All of these
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Digital radiography encompasses various technologies, including flat panel detectors (the most common), slot scanning systems (like those used in digital mammography or full-body imaging), and emerging photon-counting technologies.

---

## Q8
Question: In Mammography compression is required due to
Option A: Immobilise the breasts
Option B: Decrease scatter radiation
Option C: For homogenous Image density
Option D: All of these
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Compression in mammography is vital for multiple reasons: it holds the breast still (immobilization), reduces breast thickness (decreasing scatter and improving contrast), and spreads tissue out evenly (creating homogenous density).

## Q10
Question: Digital Tomosynthesis is used for
Option A: Breast Imaging
Option B: Inner ear imaging
Option C: Angiography
Option D: All of these

Explanation: Digital tomosynthesis is a 3D imaging technique most commonly known for breast imaging (3D mammography), but it is also applied in orthopedic, chest, and head/neck (like inner ear) imaging to separate overlapping structures.

## Q11
Question: For screening of Breasts in young women below the age of 30 years the preferred investigation is
Option A: Sonomammography
Option B: Two view film screen mammography
Option C: Ductography
Option D: CT mammography
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Young women have denser breast tissue, which makes mammography less sensitive. Ultrasound (sonomammography) is the preferred initial imaging modality to differentiate solid from cystic masses without ionizing radiation.

## Q16
Question: Which of the following is a negative contrast agent
Option A: Barium
Option B: Water
Option C: Iodine
Option D: Gadolinium
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Water acts as a negative contrast agent (radiotranslucent) in certain procedures (like CT or MRI enterography) because it has lower density than the surrounding tissues, whereas barium and iodine are positive (radiopaque) agents.

## Q18
Question: Non-ionic dimmers have a iodine to particle ratio of
Option A: 3:1
Option B: 3:2
Option C: 5:2
Option D: 6:1
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Non-ionic dimers consist of two benzene rings (each with 3 iodine atoms) linked together, yielding 6 iodine atoms per single molecule that does not dissociate in water, resulting in a 6:1 ratio, giving them the lowest osmolality.

## Q21
Question: Brightness gain as a result of reduction of image size in IITV is called
Option A: Flux gain
Option B: Minification gain
Option C: Output gain
Option D: Amplification gain
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: In an Image Intensifier (II), minification gain occurs because the same number of electrons produced at the large input phosphor are compressed into the much smaller area of the output phosphor, increasing the apparent brightness.

## Q23
Question: In modern Image Intensifiers the input fluorescent screen is made of
Option A: Zinc sulphate
Option B: Barium Sulphate
Option C: Copper Bromide
Option D: Cesium Iodide
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Modern image intensifiers use Cesium Iodide (CsI) for the input phosphor because it can be grown in tightly packed, needle-like crystals that efficiently absorb X-rays and channel the resulting light with minimal lateral spread, improving spatial resolution.

---

## Q24
Question: In wide angle tomography the tomographic arc is usually
Option A: 5-10 degrees
Option B: 10-50 degrees
Option C: 200-220 degrees
Option D: 0-5 degrees
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Wide-angle tomography uses a larger exposure angle (typically greater than 10 degrees, often up to 50 degrees) to produce a thinner focal plane, maximizing the blurring of structures outside the focal plane.

## Q32
Question: Which of the following is not a part of the gantry in CT Scan machines
Option A: Tube
Option B: Patient couch
Option C: Workstation
Option D: Detector array
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The gantry houses the X-ray tube, detector array, and high-voltage generator. The workstation (console) is separate, located in the control room where the technologist operates the scanner.

Option A: 5 degrees
Option B: 50 degrees
Option C: 80 degrees
Option D: 120 degrees

Explanation: Third-generation CT scanners use a wide fan beam, typically ranging from 30 to 60 degrees (with 50 being a common representation), wide enough to encompass the entire patient during the rotate-rotate motion.

## Q35
Question: Slip ring technology is used in
Option A: Conventional CT scanners
Option B: Axial third generation CT scanners
Option C: Spiral CT scanners
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Slip ring technology allows the continuous transmission of power and data without the use of cables, which is essential for the continuous gantry rotation required in spiral (helical) CT scanning.
