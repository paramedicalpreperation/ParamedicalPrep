## Q36
Question: The ventricles in the brain contain
Option A: CSF
Option B: Smooth muscles
Option C: Bone
Option D: Grey matter
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The ventricular system of the brain is a network of communicating cavities filled with cerebrospinal fluid (CSF), which cushions the brain and spinal cord.
## Q37
Question: Patient couch in CT scanners is made up of
Option A: Lead coated iron
Option B: Copper sheets
Option C: Carbon fiber
Option D: Amalgamated steel
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Patient couches in CT and MRI are typically made of carbon fiber because it is strong, lightweight, and highly radiolucent, meaning it does not interfere with the X-ray beam or cause artifacts.
## Q38
Question: What is the type of collimation used in CT scan
Option A: Pre-patient collimation
Option B: Post patient collimation
Option C: Both (a) & (b)
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: CT scanners utilize both pre-patient collimators to shape the beam and limit patient dose, and post-patient collimators to reduce scatter radiation reaching the detectors and to define slice thickness.
## Q39
Question: Mathematical method of image reconstruction used in CT is
Option A: Simple Back Projection
Option B: Filtered Back Projection
Option C: Fourier Reconstruction
Option D: All of these
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Filtered Back Projection (FBP) is the traditional and most widely known analytic method. However, Simple Back Projection and iterative reconstruction techniques (often involving Fourier transforms) are also fundamental mathematical methods used in CT image reconstruction.
## Q40
Question: Windowing is a method used for
Option A: Image acquisition
Option B: Image display
Option C: Image reconstruction
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Windowing (adjusting window width and level) is a post-processing technique used to manipulate the grayscale contrast and brightness of the digital image on the display monitor to optimize visualization of different tissues.
## Q41
Question: The most common type of post processing protocol used for CT Angiography is
Option A: Maximum Intensity Projection
Option B: Shaded Surface Display
Option C: Minimum Intensity Projection
Option D: Axial Surface Display
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Maximum Intensity Projection (MIP) is heavily used in CT Angiography because it projects the voxels with the highest attenuation (the contrast-enhanced blood vessels) onto a 2D plane, creating excellent angiogram-like images.
## Q42
Question: In CT Brain – hemorrhage or collection between the pia and arachnoid membrane will be
Option A: Extra-Dural collection
Option B: Sub-Dural collection
Option C: Sub-Arachnoid collection
Option D: Intra-parenchymal collection
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The subarachnoid space is anatomically located between the arachnoid mater and the pia mater; therefore, bleeding in this area is termed a subarachnoid hemorrhage (SAH).
## Q43
Question: To perform CT of the ankle joint the patient position will be
Option A: Head First Supine (HFS)
Option B: Head First Prone (HFP)
Option C: Feet First Supine (FFS)
Option D: Feet First Prone (FFP)
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: For imaging the lower extremities like the ankle, the patient is typically positioned Feet First Supine (FFS) on the couch, which is more comfortable and practical for isocentering the targeted anatomy.
## Q44
Question: The following type of contrast is used in MRI studies
Option A: Iodinated contrast agents
Option B: Gadolinium contrast agents
Option C: Barium contrast agents
Option D: All of these
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Gadolinium-based contrast agents (GBCAs) are the standard paramagnetic agents used in MRI to enhance tissue contrast by shortening the T1 relaxation time. Iodine and Barium are used in X-ray and fluoroscopy.
## Q45
Question: In T2 weighted images in MRI CSF appears
Option A: Black
Option B: Grey
Option C: White
Option D: No signal
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: On T2-weighted MRI sequences, fluids such as cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) have a long T2 relaxation time and therefore appear hyperintense, or bright white.
## Q46
Question: RF shielding in MRI magnet room is done using
Option A: Bismuth
Option B: Manganese
Option C: Copper
Option D: Lead
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The MRI room must act as a Faraday cage to prevent external radiofrequency signals from interfering with the scanner. Copper shielding is the most common material used to construct this RF enclosure.
## Q47
Question: Full form of FLAIR sequence used in MRI studies is
Option A: Fluid Level Array Inversion Recovery
Option B: Fine Level Attenuation Inversion Reaction
Option C: Fluid Level Array Inversion Reaction
Option D: Fluid Attenuation Inversion Recovery
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: FLAIR stands for Fluid Attenuation Inversion Recovery, a specialized MRI sequence that suppresses the bright signal of CSF to make periventricular brain lesions more conspicuous.
## Q48
Question: Field strength of MRI magnets is measured using which unit
Option A: Roentgen
Option B: Nikol
Option C: Sievert
Option D: Tesla
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The static magnetic field strength of an MRI scanner is measured in Tesla (T). One Tesla equals 10,000 Gauss.
## Q49
Question: The most common sequence used for performing MR angiography is :
Option A: Time of Flight
Option B: Inversion recovery
Option C: Phase encoded
Option D: Proton density
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Time-of-Flight (TOF) is a standard non-contrast MRA technique that relies on the flow of unsaturated blood entering the imaging slice to generate high signal intensity against stationary tissue.
## Q50
Question: In superconductor MRI equipments, the required critical temperature in cryostat is maintained by using
Option A: Titanium
Option B: Helium
Option C: Sulfur
Option D: Carbon dioxide
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Superconducting MRI magnets use liquid helium to cool the magnet coils to near absolute zero (approx. 4 Kelvin), removing electrical resistance and allowing the magnetic field to persist without a continuous power supply.
## Q51
Question: For personal protection in MRI room technologist should use
Option A: Iron
Option B: Lead
Option C: Aluminium
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: MRI does not use ionizing radiation, so lead aprons are unnecessary. Protection in an MRI environment involves strict adherence to safety protocols regarding ferromagnetic objects, not shielding garments.
## Q52
Question: Trans rectal coils in MRI are used for examination of
Option A: Vagina
Option B: Uterus
Option C: Prostate
Option D: Kidneys
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: An endorectal (transrectal) coil is inserted into the rectum to place the receiving antenna directly adjacent to the prostate gland, providing exceptionally high-resolution images for prostate cancer staging.
## Q53
Question: Preferred position of patient in performing MRI of Breast is
Option A: Prone
Option B: Lateral
Option C: Supine
Option D: Oblique
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Breast MRI is performed with the patient in the prone position, allowing the breasts to hang dependently into dedicated breast coils, which minimizes respiratory motion artifacts and flattens the tissue.
## Q54
Question: Which of the following is not a contraindication for undergoing MRI examination
Option A: Patient with pacemaker
Option B: Patient with joint prosthesis
Option C: Pregnant patient
Option D: All of these
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Modern non-ferromagnetic joint prostheses are generally MRI safe, but pregnancy is universally not considered an absolute contraindication (though contrast is avoided), whereas many legacy pacemakers are absolute contraindications.
## Q55
Question: In MR Enteroclysis the contrast material used is
Option A: Barium
Option B: Water
Option C: Methylcellulose
Option D: Iodine
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: In MR Enteroclysis, a biphasic oral contrast agent like methylcellulose (often mixed with water) is used because it distends the bowel loops while providing a dark signal on T1 and a bright signal on T2.
## Q56
Question: Magnetic field inhomogenicities are eliminated by a process called
Option A: Shimming
Option B: Flushing
Option C: Fractionating
Option D: Extracting
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Shimming is the process of adjusting the magnetic field using metal plates (passive) or electromagnetic coils (active) to make the main magnetic field (B0) as homogeneous as possible.
## Q57
Question: Full form of PET is
Option A: Positron Emission Tomography
Option B: Positive Excitation Tomography
Option C: Positive Emission Test
Option D: Positron Emission Test
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: PET stands for Positron Emission Tomography, a nuclear medicine functional imaging technique that observes metabolic processes in the body.
## Q58
Question: MRU is an investigation of
Option A: Heart
Option B: Kidneys
Option C: Liver
Option D: Lungs
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Magnetic Resonance Urography (MRU) is an MRI procedure optimized to evaluate the urinary tract, including the kidneys, ureters, and bladder.
## Q59
Question: The crystals used in Ultrasound probes are called
Option A: Piezo electrical crystals
Option B: Pie mechanical crystals
Option C: Pie square crystals
Option D: Piezo ceramic crystals
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Ultrasound transducers rely on piezoelectric crystals (often synthetic ceramics like PZT) which change shape in response to an electric current to produce sound waves, and vice versa.
## Q60
Question: Echocardiography is examination of
Option A: Heart
Option B: Lungs
Option C: Liver
Option D: All of these
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Echocardiography is the diagnostic use of ultrasound specifically to image the heart and its valves.
## Q61
Question: Which of the following is not a type of biopsy
Option A: Core
Option B: Extraction
Option C: Aspiration
Option D: Vacuum
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Core-needle, fine-needle aspiration (FNA), and vacuum-assisted are all standard biopsy techniques. "Extraction" is a general surgical term, not a specific biopsy category.
## Q62
Question: DSA stands for
Option A: Digital Synchronised Anatomy
Option B: Digital Subtraction Anatomy
Option C: Digital Subtraction Angiography
Option D: Digital Synchronised Angiography
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: DSA (Digital Subtraction Angiography) is a fluoroscopic technique used in interventional radiology to clearly visualize blood vessels in a bony or dense soft tissue environment by subtracting a pre-contrast image from the post-contrast image.
## Q63
Question: Gelfoam is a material commonly used for
Option A: Embolisation
Option B: Aspiration
Option C: Drainage
Option D: Coiling
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Gelfoam (absorbable gelatin sponge) is an embolic agent used in interventional radiology to temporarily occlude blood vessels to control bleeding or cut off blood supply to tumors.
## Q64
Question: Vertebroplasty is a procedure done for
Option A: Spine
Option B: Brain
Option C: Heart
Option D: Artery
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: Vertebroplasty is a minimally invasive spinal procedure in which bone cement is injected into a fractured vertebra to stabilize it and relieve pain.
## Q65
Question: Unit to measure Catheter diameter is
Option A: Meter
Option B: Angstrom
Option C: French
Option D: Sievert
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The French (Fr) scale is universally used to measure the outer diameter of catheters, needles, and other cylindrical medical instruments (1 French = 1/3 mm).
