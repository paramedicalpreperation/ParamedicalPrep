## Q1
Question: Which of the following statements about Mönckeberg’s arteriosclerosis is true?
Option A: It primarily affects the intima of large arteries
Option B: It is an example of metastatic calcification
Option C: It commonly leads to significant luminal narrowing and ischemia
Option D: It is characterized by medial calcification without significant clinical consequences
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Mönckeberg’s arteriosclerosis (medial calcific sclerosis) involves the deposition of calcium in the media of medium-sized arteries. It is a distinct process from atherosclerosis and generally does not cause significant luminal narrowing, meaning it rarely leads to clinical symptoms like ischemia.

Option A: Downward
Option B: Upward
Option C: To the left

Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Hard

## Q17
Question: Ventricular repolarization is represented on the ECG by which of the following?
Option A: P wave
Option B: PR segment
Option C: ST segment, T wave and U wave
Option D: QRS complex
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: On an ECG, the QRS complex represents ventricular depolarization. The subsequent ST segment, T wave, and U wave reflect the processes associated with ventricular repolarization (the recovery phase of the heart muscle cells).

Option A: The patient's age
Option B: Respiratory rate
Option C: The electrical position of the heart
Option D: The thickness of the chest wall
Correct Answer: Option C

## Q19
Question: In most normal ECGs, the QRS axis lies between
Option A: -30° and +100°
Option B: -90° and +120°
Option C: 0° and +180°
Option D: -60° and +60°
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The normal QRS axis in an adult is generally considered to be between -30° and +90° or sometimes up to +100°. An axis outside this range suggests axis deviation, which can indicate heart chamber enlargement or conduction abnormalities.

## Q20
Question: A QRS axis of -30° or more negative is called
Option A: Right axis deviation
Option B: Normal axis
Option C: Left axis deviation
Option D: Extreme axis deviation
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: When the QRS axis is more negative than -30° (i.e., between -30° and -90°), it is classified as Left Axis Deviation (LAD). This is commonly associated with left anterior fascicular block or left ventricular hypertrophy.

## Q24
Question: Which of the following is true regarding hinge joints (Ginglymi)?
Option A: They allow movement in multiple planes and have flat articular surfaces
Option B: They have ball-and-socket structure and allow rotational movement
Option C: They have pulley-shaped articular surfaces, strong collateral ligaments and permit movement in one plane
Option D: They allow gliding movements between flat bones without ligament support

Explanation: Hinge joints (ginglymus) act like a door hinge, permitting movement in only one plane (flexion and extension).
