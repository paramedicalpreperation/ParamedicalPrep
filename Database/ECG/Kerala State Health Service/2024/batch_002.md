## Q26
Question: The heart is located in the thoracic cavity in between the lungs in a space called
Option A: Pericardium
Option B: Ventricles
Option C: Mediastinum
Option D: Cardiac notch
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The heart is positioned in the central compartment of the thoracic cavity, known as the mediastinum, which is the space situated between the two pleural cavities containing the lungs.

## Q27
Question: Which of the following statements is correct about the function of pericardial fluid?
Option A: It acts as a lubricant between the heart and the pericardium
Option B: It helps in maintaining blood pressure
Option C: It allows flow of blood in one direction only
Option D: All of the above
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The pericardial fluid is a serous fluid located within the pericardial sac. Its primary function is to reduce friction between the beating heart and the surrounding pericardial layers, preventing irritation during cardiac movement.

## Q31
Question: In the cardiac cycle, the AV valves remain closed due to
Option A: Increasing ventricular pressure
Option B: Decreasing ventricular pressure
Option C: Increasing auricular pressure
Option D: None of the above
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: As the ventricles begin to contract (ventricular systole), the pressure inside the ventricle rises rapidly. Once this pressure exceeds the pressure in the atria (auricles), the AV valves are forced shut to prevent regurgitation.
Source: Original PYQ
## Q32
Question: Choose the correct option to complete the table (Auricular systole: AV valves - ...a..., Semilunar valves - Closed) | (Ventricular systole: AV valves - Closed, Semilunar valves - ...b...)
Option A: a-closed, b-open
Option B: a-open, b-open
Option C: a-closed, b-open
Option D: a-open, b-closed
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: During auricular (atrial) systole, the AV valves are open to allow blood flow into the ventricles. During ventricular systole, the AV valves close, and the semilunar valves must be open to allow blood to be pumped into the arteries. Thus, (a) is open and (b) is open.
Source: Original PYQ
## Q33
Question: Under what conditions does the SA node increases the number of electrical impulses and cardiac output?
Option A: Exercise
Option B: Damage from heart diseases
Option C: Stimulation of septa
Option D: All of the above
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: During physical exercise, the body demands more oxygen. The autonomic nervous system stimulates the SA node to increase its rate of depolarization (electrical impulses), resulting in a faster heart rate and increased cardiac output.
Source: Original PYQ
## Q34
Question: If the sinoatrial node fails, in a normal heart, the atrioventricular node (AV node) takes over the pacemaker function. However
Option A: Heart rate will be lower
Option B: Stroke volume will be lower
Option C: Both of the above
Option D: None of the above
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The AV node has a slower intrinsic rate of firing than the SA node. Furthermore, because atrial contraction is lost or less efficient without the SA node's input, ventricular filling decreases, leading to a reduced stroke volume and overall lower cardiac output.
Source: Original PYQ

## Q36
Question: The most appropriate investigation to diagnose mitral stenosis in a patient is
Option A: Electrocardiogram
Option B: Cardiac catheterization
Option C: Plain X-Ray of the chest
Option D: Doppler Echocardiography
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: Doppler echocardiography is the diagnostic gold standard for mitral stenosis because it allows for direct visualization of the valve leaflets and precise measurement of the pressure gradient across the stenotic valve.

## Q38
Question: The natural pacemaker of the heart is
Option A: Bundle of His
Option B: AV node
Option C: SA node
Option D: Purkinje fibers
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: The Sinoatrial (SA) node is considered the natural pacemaker because it possesses the highest intrinsic rate of automaticity, setting the basic rhythm for the entire heart.

Question: Which among the following is used to give shock to a patient with ventricular fibrillation?
Option A: Defibrillator
Option B: Cardio scope
Option C: Pacemaker
Option D: Ventilator
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: A defibrillator is the clinical device specifically used to deliver a high-energy electrical shock to the heart, which terminates ventricular fibrillation and allows the heart to resume a normal rhythm.
