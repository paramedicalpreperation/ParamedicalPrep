## Q31
Question: In the cardiac cycle, the AV valves remain closed due to
Option A: Increasing ventricular pressure
Option B: Decreasing ventricular pressure
Option C: Increasing auricular pressure
Option D: None of the above
Correct Answer: Option A
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: As the ventricles begin to contract (ventricular systole), the pressure inside the ventricle rises rapidly. Once this pressure exceeds the pressure in the atria (auricles), the AV valves are forced shut to prevent regurgitation.

## Q32
Question: Choose the correct option to complete the table (Auricular systole: AV valves - ...a..., Semilunar valves - Closed) | (Ventricular systole: AV valves - Closed, Semilunar valves - ...b...)
Option A: a-closed, b-open
Option B: a-open, b-open
Option C: a-closed, b-open
Option D: a-open, b-closed
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: During auricular (atrial) systole, the AV valves are open to allow blood flow into the ventricles. During ventricular systole, the AV valves close, and the semilunar valves must be open to allow blood to be pumped into the arteries. Thus, (a) is open and (b) is open.
Source: Original PYQ

Option A: Exercise

Option D: All of the above

Explanation: During physical exercise, the body demands more oxygen. The autonomic nervous system stimulates the SA node to increase its rate of depolarization (electrical impulses), resulting in a faster heart rate and increased cardiac output.

## Q34
Question: If the sinoatrial node fails, in a normal heart, the atrioventricular node (AV node) takes over the pacemaker function. However
Option A: Heart rate will be lower
Option B: Stroke volume will be lower
Option C: Both of the above
Option D: None of the above
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The AV node has a slower intrinsic rate of firing than the SA node. Furthermore, because atrial contraction is lost or less efficient without the SA node's input, ventricular filling decreases, leading to a reduced stroke volume and overall lower cardiac output.
Source: Original PYQ

## Q49
Question: In cardioversion the electric shock is given
Option A: At the beginning of P wave
Option B: At the peak or just after R wave
Option C: At the peak of T wave
Option D: After T wave
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: In synchronized cardioversion, the shock must be delivered during the R wave of the QRS complex to avoid the vulnerable period of the T wave. Shocking during the T wave could accidentally induce ventricular fibrillation.
Source: Original PYQ
