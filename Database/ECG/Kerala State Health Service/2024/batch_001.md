## Q1
Question: The haemoglobin of RBC after 120 days of lifespan gets converted first into
Option A: Bilirubin
Option B: Cytokinin
Option C: Cytochrome
Option D: Biliverdin
Correct Answer: Option D
Difficulty: Easy
Explanation: When red blood cells complete their 120-day lifespan, they are broken down by macrophages. The heme group is degraded, initially forming biliverdin, which is then rapidly converted into bilirubin.

## Q2
Question: The volume of air used by a man to fill a balloon is
Option A: IRV
Option B: ERV
Option C: TV
Option D: RV
Correct Answer: Option B
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The Expiratory Reserve Volume (ERV) is the additional volume of air that can be forcefully exhaled after a normal tidal expiration. This forced exhalation is the mechanism used to fill a balloon.
Source: Original PYQ
## Q3
Question: At which point in ECG the repolarisation of atrium occurs
Option A: P wave
Option B: T wave
Option C: QRS wave
Option D: None of these
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Hard
Explanation: Atrial repolarisation occurs simultaneously with ventricular depolarisation. Since the ventricular depolarisation event (QRS complex) has much greater electrical magnitude, it masks the atrial repolarisation signal on the surface ECG.
Source: Original PYQ

## Q12
Question: The pulmonary semilunar valve prevents back flow of blood into the
Option A: Right atrium
Option B: Left atrium
Option C: Right ventricle
Option D: Left ventricle
Correct Answer: Option C
Difficulty: Medium
Explanation: The pulmonary semilunar valve is located between the right ventricle and the pulmonary artery. It closes during ventricular diastole to prevent blood from flowing backward into the right ventricle.

## Q21
Question: The reason for delay in travel of cardiac impulse from atria to ventricles is to
Option A: Allows time for the atria to empty their blood into the ventricles before ventricular contraction begins
Option B: Allows time for the ventricles to empty their blood
Option C: To minimize stroke volume
Option D: None of the above
